# CANON NOTES — TESSA KAEL

Authoritative reference for the Tessa storyline after the integration /
oomph expansion pass. Any scene touching Tessa, the Hand Count, Ward-9,
Rhea Kael, the medbay's pre-dawn ritual, the Three Categories, or the
EMP / OB thesis split must be consistent with this file. If a scene
contradicts this file, the scene is wrong.

Last updated: 2026-05-06 (Tessa integration pass).

---

## 1. CORE FACTS

- **Tessa Kael.** Field medic and bio-mechanic. Phoenix's emotional
  ballast. Closest friend in the rebellion: Zira. Anchor LI for one of
  the EMP-path branches.
- **Tessa was born in the working tier of the Foundry Ward** and lost
  her mother in the Ward-9 blackout when she was fourteen.
- **Her mother was Rhea Kael** — a labor-tier worker who carried, before
  her death, three names she'd failed to save before Ward-9. Those three
  names become canon in this document and in the EMP thesis scene.
- **Tessa's left hand bears a burn scar** from Ward-9. The scar is
  visible if her sleeve is pushed past the wrist on the inside of the
  forearm. The scar's origin: she held a buckled support brace in place
  long enough for her mother to speak. The brace's heat-fail point
  burned through the fabric and the skin under it. The hand stayed
  because pain told it to leave.
- **Tessa runs a private ritual called *the Hand Count.*** It is named
  in canon notes only. No character names it aloud.
- **The Hand Count has been running for fifteen years** as of Act 4. It
  began at her mother's bedside. It has continued — in convoy tents,
  labor-clinic cells, every Phoenix base — every dawn before her shift,
  alone, no witness. It leaves no trace. It exists only while she is
  saying it.
- **The medbay is the Hand Count's home.** Tessa always opens the
  medbay before any other personnel arrive. The opening is the count.
  The triage that follows is the work she does after grief has been
  accounted for.

## 2. THE HAND COUNT

### 2.1 What it is

> *Tessa says each name with the last truth her hands knew about
> them.*

That sentence is the whole rule. Each entry is one name plus one to
three short clinical details — the body-truth Tessa was holding when
the person became history. The cadence is medical, not lyrical:

- **One detail** — most entries.
- **Two details** — entries with weight beyond the standard.
- **Three details** — the most load-bearing entries (her mother is
  the only canon-locked three-detail entry).

The ritual sounds like a medic checking vitals on the dead. Not a
prayer. Not a memorial protocol. A vital-check on people whose vitals
no longer move.

**Important register note:** Zira's nine names are architectural —
salt, cryptographic anchor, infrastructure. Tessa's count is
respiratory — voice, scar tissue, breath. The two LIs share a
"forged-in-catastrophe-at-fourteen-or-fifteen" pair anchor (see §11)
but their memorials sit at opposite poles. Zira's grief leaves
traces in every Ghostline packet. Tessa's grief disappears at the end
of every recitation.

### 2.2 The structure of the count (every dawn)

The count is said in chronological order. It always opens with the
inheritance.

**The inheritance — Rhea Kael's three:**

```
te "Maren Holt. Didn't reach."
te "Siva Renn. Didn't reach."
te "Alo Corren. Didn't reach."
```

These three names are not Tessa's patients. They are people Rhea Kael
failed to save before Ward-9. Rhea spoke them at the bedside as
regret, apology, unfinished grief. Tessa, fourteen, traumatized, and
trying to survive the meaning of what she just witnessed, turned the
regret into a rule. The misreading is the wound.

**Rhea's own entry — the first name Tessa added:**

```
te "Rhea Kael. Left hand. Burned brace. Breath shallow."
```

The only canon-locked three-detail entry. The "left hand" detail is
Rhea's, not Tessa's — Rhea reached for Tessa with her left hand at
the end. *Burned brace* is the support brace Tessa was holding. *Breath
shallow* is the last vital Tessa took before her mother stopped
speaking. Rhea's category is **Gone.**

**The accumulating count — patients Tessa has been the last hand to
touch (chronological):**

Each gets one to two details. Examples (canon-locking representative
entries; the full count is long by Act 4 and not all of it is spoken
on screen):

```
te "Torin Vell. Two fingers. Pulse gone."
te "Joran Tev. Chest wound. Still warm."
te "Davel Ostra. Right hand. Asked for water."
te "Liora Rylan. Breath held. Breath returned."
```

**Aeron's entry:**

```
te "Aeron Rylan. Forearm gash. Pulse held. Returned."
```

Anchored to `a3_s20a_scar_and_steady_emp` — Aeron's checkpoint-scramble
gash, treated by Tessa, the Brand-scar reveal scene. *Pulse held* is
the body-truth Tessa kept from that night: his heart rate did not
spike when she pushed his sleeve past the Brand. The not-spike is
its own telling. The category is **Returned.**

### 2.3 The Three Categories

Tessa never names the categories aloud. The structure is private.
Aeron may infer one if the count is exposed long enough.

- **Gone** — patients who died under her hands. The largest category
  by Act 4. Includes Rhea Kael, Torin Vell, Joran Tev, Davel Ostra.
  Standard recitation form: name plus one to two body-details.
- **Returned** — patients she nearly lost but brought back. The body
  was on the threshold; her hands held the line. Includes Liora
  Rylan (breath held / breath returned) and Aeron Rylan (forearm
  gash, pulse held). Recitation form usually closes with a positive
  body-detail (*pulse held, breath returned*).
- **Unfinished** — patients whose outcome Tessa still carries.
  Triage cases where she had to choose, and the person she did not
  reach for died, but the person she did reach for survived in a
  state that left the math unfinished. Or: patients whose treatment
  was interrupted by an evacuation or raid and Tessa never learned
  the outcome. Recitation form has no closing detail — the entry
  ends on the body-truth she had at the moment of interruption.
  *(Unfinished entries are mostly off-screen background for now;
  any scene that wants to add one should consult this document.)*

### 2.4 Who knows about the count before the EMP thesis scene

**Nobody.**

Not Selene. Not Lyra. Not Zira (she will half-hear it during the
pair beat — see §6 — but she does not understand its full structure).
Not Aeron. Not the patients' families. Not the rebellion's records.

Every patient's family has the public board. Phoenix has the public
board. The board has been the official record for fifteen years
across multiple base moves. The Hand Count has been the private
parallel — said aloud only by Tessa, only to herself, only at dawn,
in the empty medbay before anyone else arrives.

**The fragility is the load-bearing thematic claim of the Tessa
arc:** Tessa's memorial is the only one in the rebellion that leaves
no trace. If she dies, the count dies with her. There is no archive,
no document, no list. The names exist only while she is saying them.

This is not Zira's broadcast (which gets re-encrypted and stored).
This is not Noelle's authorship (which is recorded in regime files
and Phoenix counter-records). Tessa's grief lives only in the living
body that carries it.

## 3. WARD-9

### 3.1 What happened

The Ward-9 blackout. Tessa was fourteen. The labor-tier housing block
where Rhea Kael lived suffered a structural collapse during a regime
power-shutdown maintenance window — the regime had cut emergency
generator backup to non-priority blocks for a forty-eight-hour
window without informing the residents. A support failure during
that window dropped a section of ceiling onto the lower floor, where
Rhea was working an extended shift at home.

The collapse trapped Rhea under a buckled support brace. The brace
did not crush her outright; it pinned her chest. Her breathing was
compromised but possible. She had perhaps fifteen to twenty minutes
before the brace would fail completely and the rest of the section
would come down.

Tessa was in the next room. She got to her mother. She did not have
the strength to lift the brace. **She held it in place.** The brace's
heat-fail point — a hot section of buckled metal — was directly under
her left hand. She held it for the duration of her mother's last
fifteen minutes. The burn went through the fabric and the skin.

Rhea did not survive. The brace failed shortly after Rhea stopped
speaking. The rest of the section came down. Tessa was not under it.
She had moved by then because Rhea told her to.

Tessa's left hand has carried the scar since.

### 3.2 What Rhea said

Rhea spent her last minutes giving Tessa the three names — people
she had failed to reach during her own life as a labor-tier worker.
These were not Ward-9 victims. They were earlier failures. Rhea had
been carrying them privately and had never told her daughter about
them.

She named them in order:

> *Maren Holt. Didn't reach.*
> *Siva Renn. Didn't reach.*
> *Alo Corren. Didn't reach.*

Then, the load-bearing instruction:

> *Say who your hands remember.*
> *Or the world gets to call them gone.*

**Rhea's intent was not to give Tessa a rule.** Rhea was a dying
woman releasing the names she had not been able to release while
living. She was unburdening, not instructing.

**Tessa, fourteen, took it as instruction.** That is the wound.
Tessa has been keeping the count for fifteen years on the
assumption that her mother's last words were a directive. They were
a confession.

Tessa does not know this. She may never know this. The wound's
generative engine is the misreading.

### 3.3 "The hand that stayed"

The convoy that pulled Tessa out of Ward-9 named her *the hand that
stayed* because she had stayed beside her mother for the full
fifteen minutes. The convoy intended praise.

**Tessa hears it as indictment.** *The hand stayed* because she did
not have the strength to do anything else with it. *The hand stayed*
means it did not reach for whoever might have been in the next room.
*The hand stayed* is two-faced canonically and the deepening pass
respects both faces simultaneously.

The "hand that stayed" line of canon (already in `27_Tessa_Kael`
profile) now resolves: the hand stayed because pain told it to
leave and Tessa overrode the pain. The override is what defined
her medical career.

## 4. THE BURN SCAR

- **Location:** inside of the left forearm, lower third — the
  contact point where the buckled brace's heat-fail seam pressed
  against her skin while she held it. Roughly the size of two
  thumbprints.
- **Texture:** old, well-healed, slightly raised. The skin is paler
  than the surrounding forearm. Visible if the sleeve is pushed
  past the wrist far enough.
- **Tessa rarely lets anyone see it.** Her medbay sleeves are
  always at full length. She works with rolled sleeves only when
  she is alone.
- **Aeron has not seen the scar yet.** The Brand-scar reveal in
  `a3_s20a_scar_and_steady_emp` was about *his* scar, not hers. The
  Tessa thesis EMP scene is the scene where Aeron sees Tessa's
  burn for the first time — pushed-up sleeves while she's saying
  the count, before she realizes he is in the doorway.
- **The scar is canonically respected** in any future Tessa scene
  where her left forearm is in frame. If a scene shows the
  forearm and does not include the scar, the scene is wrong.

## 5. THE EMP THESIS — THE HAND COUNT WITNESSED

**Scene:** `a4_s??_tessa_hand_count_emp.rpy` (numbering TBD; placement
mid-Act-4 EMP, after the implant surgery and before the deepening
erotic).

### 5.1 Setup

Aeron has been arriving at the medbay at a regular early-morning
hour for six weeks of base operations — usually for a brief check
with Tessa before the day-shift begins. His arrival timing is
muscle memory. **One morning his arrival is wrong by twelve
minutes.** Earlier than usual. He has come from a no-sleep Selene
briefing and he wants to see Tessa before he sees anyone else.

He arrives. The medbay door is unlocked. The lights are on at
half. Tessa is at the supply cart with her back to the door. Her
sleeves are rolled. Her left forearm is visible.

She is saying the count.

### 5.2 The reveal

Aeron stays in the doorway. He does not announce himself. He has
walked into something he understands, in the part of his head that
does the understanding before language, is not for him to interrupt.

He hears the inheritance:

```
te "Maren Holt. Didn't reach."
te "Siva Renn. Didn't reach."
te "Alo Corren. Didn't reach."
```

He hears Rhea:

```
te "Rhea Kael. Left hand. Burned brace. Breath shallow."
```

He hears the count proper. Names he doesn't know. Names he
half-knows. Then names he knows. Then a name that lands hard —
Liora's entry — and he makes a small involuntary sound.

Tessa hears the sound. She does not turn.

She finishes the sequence to the present — meaning she keeps going
to where the count stops. The count stops on Aeron.

```
te "Aeron Rylan—"
```

She stops there.

### 5.3 The exchange

Aeron speaks once.

```
a "You can finish it."
```

Tessa does not turn.

```
te "No."
te "Not while you're standing there."
```

Aeron does not push. He takes one step into the medbay, sits on the
edge of the nearest empty bed, and waits.

She does not finish the count out loud. She finishes it in her
head. He sits while she does.

When she turns to face him, her sleeves are still rolled. The scar
is visible. He has not asked about it.

The conversation that follows is not the count being explained. It
is the conversation between two people who now share an asymmetry:
he has heard her ritual; she has not heard his (and there isn't
one, not in the same shape — Aeron's ledger of women's sentences
from `a4_s08` is the closest equivalent). She tells him about
Rhea. About the brace. About her mother's three names. About the
misreading — though she does not call it a misreading, because she
does not know it is one.

He listens. He does not fix anything. He does not promise to
remember the names with her. He does not ask to be removed from
the count. He asks one question:

```
a "What category am I."
```

And Tessa, who has never named the categories aloud:

```
te "Returned."
```

That is the scene's load. Aeron understands he has been carried not
as a death but as a return. The category is the gift.

The scene closes on him sitting on the bed and her at the supply
cart, sleeves still rolled. Both of them quiet. The count is over
for the day. The next day's count will include his sound, and his
question, and the way he didn't push, but those will not be said
aloud — they will be in the body-detail of his next entry, if
there is one, on a day Tessa decides to add it.

## 6. THE OB THESIS — THE MEDBAY GOES SILENT

**Scene:** `a4_s??_tessa_silent_medbay_ob.rpy` (numbering TBD;
placement after `a4_s16_tessa_fracture_point_ob`).

### 6.1 Setup

Same dawn arrival pattern, OB calibration. Aeron has been arriving
at the medbay early on this path too — but the arrivals are
operational, not personal. He comes to confirm casualty counts and
to read the board.

This morning he arrives at the same wrong-by-twelve-minutes timing
as the EMP scene. The scene's discipline: **same room, same time,
same Aeron.** The path divergence is what's missing.

### 6.2 The collapse

Aeron arrives. The medbay door is unlocked. The lights are on at
half. Tessa is at the board with her back to the door. Her
sleeves are rolled — same as EMP. Her left forearm is visible.

**She is not saying the count.**

Not skipping a name. Not stumbling. Not saying any of it. The
ritual is gone.

Aeron does not know the count exists. He does not know what is
missing. He notices only that the medbay is silent and that
Tessa is not moving — she is standing at the board with her hand
flat on the LIVING column the way the OB fracture-point scene
established (`a4_s16`), but tonight there is no peeling, no
header overwrite, no act of resistance. She is just standing.

The narrator's job is to let the player feel the absence of
something they have only seen named in canon notes. The player
does not need the count explained. The player needs the silence
to land.

Three lines carry it:

```
"At dawn, the medbay is silent."
"Not quiet. Silent."
"The names do not come."
```

### 6.3 What happens between them

Aeron does not understand the silence. He registers it as Tessa
having a quiet morning. He says good morning. She says good
morning. They run through the casualty review. Her hands are
steady. The board is clean. The work is efficient.

He leaves. He does not know what he just did not see.

**The scene's load is on the player, not the characters.** The
player who has read the canon notes knows what the silence is.
The player who has not read the canon notes will recognize the
silence on a second playthrough or on a callback in a later
scene. The OB thesis is a wound the OB Aeron does not know he
caused. That is the OB calibration of every Aeron-LI scene in
this pass.

### 6.4 Why the count broke

The breakage isn't a single event. It's the slow accretion of
fifteen months under OB-Aeron's command. Tessa has not been able
to keep the count clean because the operational cadence of OB
Phoenix has been overwriting the dawn window. She has been waking
to briefings. The medbay opens before she does. The
casualty-board updates happen on Selene's schedule, not Tessa's.

By the morning of the silent scene, the count has been getting
shorter for weeks. Names have been dropping. Tessa has not
noticed — the dropping has happened at the level of habit, not
decision.

On the silent morning she opens her mouth to start the
inheritance, gets to *Maren Holt* in her head, and the name does
not come out. The brace inside her chest that has held the
ritual for fifteen years has finally bent the wrong way under
the weight of OB-Aeron's command.

She closes her mouth. She does not try again. The board is
waiting.

### 6.5 The OB-specific pain

OB-Aeron has not made Tessa cruel. He has made her *efficient
enough to abandon her private act of mercy.*

That is the scene's knife. Tessa is not broken. Tessa is
optimized. The optimization has cost her the ritual that made
her recognizable to herself.

The path-mirror to Zira's not-waking-Aeron is exact: in both
cases, the OB withholding is structural, the EMP is a witnessed
revelation, and the OB Aeron does not know what he has cost.

## 7. THE TESSA-ZIRA PAIR BEAT — *Breath and Static*

**Scene:** `game/act3/emp/a3_int_??_tessa_zira_breath_emp.rpy`
(or path-shared interlude — see §7.1; numbering TBD; placement
late Act 3 EMP only). Brief — under 200 lines.

### 7.1 Path scope

The pair beat is **EMP only.** On OB the friendship has the
distance the OB path imposes on every closeness; the *Breath and
Static* moment doesn't happen because Tessa's count has already
started fraying by then. EMP-only is a real path-cost in either
direction.

### 7.2 What happens

A supply shortage during a base shift has pushed the medbay and
Zira's workshop into shared resource queueing. Zira walks into
the medbay before dawn for a Ghostline component (a sterile
silver-foil packet from Tessa's surgical supply) Tessa has on
loan from her own kit.

Tessa is mid-count. Zira hears the cadence before she registers
the scene. She enters quietly. She does not announce.

Tessa stops. Zira does not ask what she stopped. Zira finds the
foil packet on the second cart, picks it up, holds it for a beat
before leaving.

At the door Zira says one name back. A Ghostrunner Tessa lost six
months ago — someone Zira knew through Ghostline before Tessa
knew them through the medbay. The body-detail Zira gives is one
Tessa does not have:

```
z "Halix's nephew. Reroute relay went dark before he did."
```

Zira leaves.

### 7.3 What the moment costs Tessa

Tessa adds three positions to the count that night.

**One** is the Ghostrunner — already in the count, but now with a
second body-detail Zira gave her (*reroute relay went dark*),
which Tessa folds into the entry. The count for that name now
has the dual body-truth of two people who carried him.

**Two** is Zira herself — Tessa is now carrying Zira in the
count. Not as a death. Not as a Returned. As **Unfinished.**
Zira is the first Unfinished entry that is a person Tessa fears
losing rather than a person whose outcome Tessa never learned.
The category quietly mutates.

**Three** is the unnamed third — Tessa adds a placeholder for
the moment of being witnessed. Not a person. A position. The
position is the first piece of the count that is for an event,
not a body. Tessa does not know what to do with this. She leaves
the position empty. She will name it later if she ever names
it.

### 7.4 What the moment costs Zira

Zira understands what she walked into. She does not understand
the structure — she heard maybe twenty names, no categories, no
pattern she could parse without more data — but she understands
the *kind of thing* she walked into.

Zira does not say the name back as a gift. She says it back
because the name was inside her own carry already and being in
the room with someone else's carry made the doubling unbearable
to keep silent. The act is not Zira's compassion. It is Zira's
own count surfacing for the first time — the count that has only
existed in the salt and the chip's voice carrier.

**For the first time in eleven years, Zira has spoken a name out
loud that she has been keeping in cryptographic memorial.** The
moment is asymmetric — Tessa has been speaking her count for
fifteen years; Zira has been encrypting hers for eleven; they
brush each other's structure for one minute and neither of them
talks about it.

### 7.5 Why this is the EMP friendship beat

The Noelle-Zira pair anchor (*Verdant Decrypt*) is structural —
they hand each other the architecture of their compromised work.

The Tessa-Zira pair anchor (*Breath and Static*) is bodily —
they hand each other a name that didn't have a body present at
its loss, and a body that didn't have a name present at its
holding. The ledger and the hands meet in a doorway. Neither
closes the gap.

The pair anchor name *Breath and Static* (already locked in the
character profile) finally earns its title in this scene.

## 10. PATH EXPRESSIONS

### 10.1 EMP path

- The Hand Count exists, runs every dawn, gets longer over time,
  and is witnessed once by Aeron in the thesis scene. It is also
  half-witnessed by Zira in the *Breath and Static* pair beat.
- Tessa's left forearm scar is shown to Aeron in the thesis
  scene. From that scene forward, EMP-Tessa rolls her sleeves
  in his presence sometimes. Not always. The choice is hers.
- Aeron's category in the count is **Returned.** The category is
  spoken to him in the thesis scene exchange. The exchange is
  the gift.
- The deepening erotic (`a4_s20`) plays after the thesis. The
  reversal of care has new weight because Aeron is in the count
  not as a death-to-be but as a return.

### 10.2 OB path
