# CANON NOTES — KAEL RYLAN

Authoritative reference for the Kael storyline after the survival retcon.
Any scene touching Kael, Rava, Soren, Stillwater, or the Lower Spans chain
must be consistent with this file. If a scene contradicts this file, the
scene is wrong.

Last updated: 2026-04-14 (Kael canon reconciliation pass).

---

## 1. CORE FACTS

- **Kael Rylan** — Aeron's younger brother. Two years younger than Selene.
- **Born:** 355 AC. **Dies:** 381 AC at age 26, at Stillwater.
- **Official record (Aeries):** died by suicide at age 17, jumping from the
  Aeries rooftop. Marcus buried an empty box.
- **Actual fate:** survived the rooftop jump. Lived nine more years as
  Selene's second-in-command. Killed at Operation Stillwater.
- **Marcus does not know Kael survived.** This is load-bearing. Kael chose
  to let the record stand — "a dead man cannot be caged."

## 2. THE ROOFTOP FALL (372 AC)

- Kael was 17. Selene was 17 (technically 19 under strict age math, but
  the rooftop scene in a1 says Selene was 17; we accept a two-year drift
  on this one beat as pre-existing canon).
- The Aeries sits above the cloud line. The fall was miles long through
  the city's vertical structure.
- Kael hit, in sequence: the cloud layer, the upper industrial gantries,
  a disused maintenance span on Tier 9, the outer cables of a mid-level
  cargo crossing, the edge of a condenser shell, the side of a waste-trunk
  before it angled into the Middle Levels.
- Every impact broke something. Every impact decelerated him. His body
  became the brake on his own fall.
- Selene was running a courier route two levels above the final impact
  site. She heard the echo travel down the cable tree. She traced the
  sound. She found him in twisted structural cabling — broken in six
  places, still breathing.
- She dragged him into a maintenance tunnel and kept him alive for three
  weeks with stolen supplies and one physician she never put in a report.
- By the time he was ambulatory, Marcus had already filed the suicide
  record and buried an empty box.

## 3. THE NINE YEARS (372–381 AC)

- Kael became Selene's best second. Every doctrine of shared command that
  Selene teaches Aeron in Act IV came from Kael first. She inherited it;
  she did not invent it.
- Kael met **Rava** in 378 AC in the Foundry Wards laundry collective.
- They married in the Phoenix internal registry (no official record).
- **Soren** was born in 379 AC. Old Tongue name meaning "to hold what will
  break you." She is eleven in 390 AC.
- Kael secretly reached out to **Liora** (courier network operator) at
  some point in this window without telling Selene. Liora knew about
  Soren. Selene did not know that Liora knew.
- By 381 AC, Rava was considering leaving — she did not want Soren raised
  inside a war. Kael could not leave the war. The marriage was a slow
  tension he was living inside. Selene witnessed this tension without
  ever hearing Rava's name spoken in the war room.

## 4. OPERATION STILLWATER (381 AC)

- Formerly called Operation Verdant in older drafts. Renamed to avoid
  collision with Verdant bloodline lore. **Any reference to "Operation
  Verdant" in any remaining file is a bug.**
- Riverbank extraction in the third district.
- Selene's plan gave Kael an eleven-minute window. The actual window was
  nine minutes. Selene did not know until it was too late.
- Kael was shot twice on the bank. Ninety seconds between rounds.
- Between the rounds, on the comms, Kael said: *"Selene. I would have
  taken the nine-minute window if you had told me it was nine."*
- Selene told him she would carry it. Kael said: *"No. You do not get to
  carry it alone. It was our operation. If you try to carry it alone, you
  will make the same mistake on the next one, because the mistake was
  that you thought the clock was only mine to put on the board."*
- Kael's final line, which Selene has been carrying for nine years:
  *"The weight belongs to more than one person. Agree that it does.
  That is the promise."*
- Selene said yes into a dead channel. Marcus was standing behind her
  and heard her say it but never acknowledged it.
- Kael died at 26. Selene was 28 in the command post. Marcus's hand was
  on the back of her chair.

## 5. RAVA AND SOREN AFTER STILLWATER

- After Kael's death, Rava left the Phoenix base. She took Soren with her.
- Rava relocated to an Unders community in Sector 10. She believed that
  raising Soren inside the war was the thing Kael had refused to refuse,
  and she was unwilling to make the same refusal.
- **Selene did not know where they went.** She believed — for nine years
  — that Rava and Soren had died in the chaos following Stillwater.
  This belief is what she carries into every Kael-adjacent beat in Acts
  1–3 and the first half of Act 4.

## 6. OPERATION 391 — SECTOR 10 SWEEP (383 AC)

- Aeron's early-career sweep of Sector 10. On-camera in `a1_s23_the_sweep`.
- Rava was killed in this sweep. Aeron did not know who she was. The
  sweep was orders. The kill was one of many.
- **Aeron unknowingly killed his brother's wife in Act I.** This is the
  single most devastating retroactive fact in the game.
- Soren (age 4 at the time, 383 AC) was not with Rava at the moment of
  the kill. She had been hidden by a neighbor. A courier from Liora's
  network extracted her afterward.

## 7. THE LOWER SPANS ENCOUNTER (383 AC, pre-Operation 391)

- In `a1_s20_lower_spans`, Aeron encounters a small child between two
  pipes in the Lower Spans of Sector 10. Scene flags:
  `reassured_child` vs `ignored_child`.
- **That child is Soren.** Age 4.
- Soren does not recognize Aeron by sight. Rava had been warning her
  about Echelon soldiers. Before leaving her alone that morning, Rava
  had said something like: *"Some soldiers still got hearts. If one
  stops and looks you in the face, you remember his face."*
- Rava recognized Aeron by family resemblance to Kael. She did not make
  contact — she was protecting Soren. Rava had seen Aeron once or twice
  on Sector 10 patrols before that day, and had privately decided Aeron
  had Kael's jawline and his mother's eyes. She never told Soren who he
  was. She only told her: *remember his face.*
- Soren's memory of the encounter is a small child's memory — a soldier
  who stopped, or a soldier who didn't. That memory is all she has to
  test Aeron with in Act V.

## 8. THE LIORA CHAIN

- **EMP path:** Liora is killed in the Act 3 EMP climax. Her final
  courier message was sent before her death and reaches Selene several
  days later. That message is what Selene delivers to Aeron in
  `a4_s10a_liora_sent_word_emp`. The message contains: Rava died in
  Sector 10 (Operation 391, Aeron's sweep). Soren survived. Soren is
  alive in the Outlands. Liora has been protecting her.
- **OB path:** Liora is alive through Act 4 and Act 5. She holds the
  information herself and decides when and whether to grant Aeron a
  meeting. The OB reveal is gated behind Aeron's behavior toward Liora
  across the back half of Act 4.

## 9. THE ACT V MEETING

- EMP path: Selene delivers Aeron to Soren. Full reveal is gated behind
  whether Aeron chose `reassured_child` in `a1_s20` AND whether Aeron
  remembers the encounter when Soren tests him. Memory gate is EMP-only.
- OB path: Liora grants a narrow meeting. Soren tests Aeron with the
  Lower Spans memory. Same gate (reassured_child + remembered).
- **The gate is not "did Aeron do the right thing." The gate is "did
  Aeron do the right thing AND can he remember he did it." Memory is
  the full test.**

## 10. LOAD-BEARING QUOTES (do not paraphrase)

- Kael's final line: *"The weight belongs to more than one person.
  Agree that it does. That is the promise."*
- Kael's survival verdict: *"If the fall didn't kill me, the cage
  wasn't supposed to hold me."*
- Selene's framing: *"I am only the first page of the book."*
- Rava's instruction to Soren: *"Some soldiers still got hearts. If
  one stops and looks you in the face, you remember his face."*
