# CANON NOTES — NOELLE KORR

Authoritative reference for the Noelle storyline after the integration / trauma
expansion pass. Any scene touching Noelle, Halen, Director Faber, the Liora
file, the NDI baseline, the implant, the child file, or Protocol Verdant must
be consistent with this file. If a scene contradicts this file, the scene is
wrong.

Last updated: 2026-05-05 (Noelle integration pass).

---

## 1. CORE FACTS

- **Noelle Korr** — former CAD researcher; defected 382 AC; age 28 at story
  start. Pre-existing canon (`25_Noelle_Korr_Character_Profile`) remains
  authoritative for psychology, voice, visual profile, and base relationship
  shapes. This document **adds canon**; it does not replace it.
- Her surname **Korr** carries two etymological loads, both intentional:
  Greek *kore* (the maiden whose taking begins the world's grief — Persephone)
  and Latin *cor* (heart). Lyra recognizes this aloud once (see §13.4).
- Four pieces of new canon sit underneath Noelle's existing arc and
  recontextualize it: **Halen (the partner)**, **the implant**, **the Liora
  file**, **the NDI baseline (parents)**. These are not new arcs — they are
  the floor under what was already there.

## 2. HALEN — THE DELETED PARTNER

- **Halen** was a CAD researcher, Noelle's partner from approximately
  379–381 AC. Gender-neutral name; play either reading.
- The name carries a Welsh root meaning *salt* — the residue of tears,
  the preservation that outlives a body. The regime preserved Halen's
  "transfer" record long after the body was gone; Noelle's grief is
  what was left of the love after the editing took everything else.
- Echelon killed Halen in 381 AC. The official record is a transfer: *"reassigned
  to Outer Wards Resonance Research Station, indefinite term."*
- **The killing was operational, not personal.** Noelle's emotional
  attachment was reducing her productivity. CAD removed the cause to preserve
  the asset. Director Faber signed the operational order. (See §7.)
- After the killing, CAD performed a memory edit on Noelle to remove the
  emotional weight of the relationship. The procedural record of working
  together remained — joint paper authorship, shared housing logs, a
  co-signed expense submission for a coffee press. The *feeling* was taken;
  the *records* were preserved.
- **The transfer fiction was the containment measure.** A Noelle who thinks
  her partner is alive elsewhere does not search. A Noelle who knows they
  are dead might. The lie was maintained for over a year.
- Noelle does not discover the death until the Defection Coda (`a4_s10b`,
  see §11). The records on her data crystal show "Halen — Outer Wards
  Resonance, year four of assignment" until she cross-checks the dispatch
  manifests and finds no Halen ever arrived at any Outer Wards station.
- The discovery has two beats: first that she was edited (already canon),
  second that the edit was hiding a murder (new). The second beat is the
  scene's load.
- **Halen is dead. Halen stays dead.** No late-reveal return. The partner is
  not in the rebellion under another name. The horror is that the love is
  unrecoverable on every axis.

### 2.1 Structural rhyme with Kael

- Marcus authored a death that was not real (Kael's "suicide"). Echelon
  authored an absence that was a death (Halen's "transfer"). Twin lies,
  mirrored direction.
- The two lies are the regime's two grammars: **one for blood, one for
  assets.** Marcus protected what he loved by burying it; Echelon protected
  what it owned by maintaining a fiction of life around a corpse.

## 3. THE IMPLANT

- Noelle has a passive monitoring device implanted at the base of her
  skull. Standard CAD-issue for upper-tier researchers handling classified
  cognitive material. Designation: **Aether Tag, Researcher-Class** (internal
  CAD shorthand: "the leash"). Noelle did not know it was there.
- The implant transmits at low duty cycle on a CAD-internal band Ghostline
  cannot easily intercept. It does not record audio. It logs:
  - Coarse location (sector-level, not building-level).
  - Resonance signature (which would identify her uniquely if anyone scanned
    for her tag).
  - Aggregate cognitive-load data via passive Band coupling.
- **Tessa finds the implant during medical intake when Noelle joins the
  rebellion.** This is the Tessa–Noelle follow-up anchor (see §11). Tessa
  is the only person besides Noelle herself who has ever known about it.
- **Decision point handled in the anchor scene:**
  - **EMP path:** Tessa removes the implant. Risky surgery; nerve damage
    possible; Noelle awake for it because anesthesia would suppress
    cognitive activity in a way the implant might flag.
  - **OB path:** Noelle chooses to leave the implant active and feed false
    intel back. *"Closed-loop information control. We control what they
    see."* This works for several months. It is also why some of OB
    Aeron's operations are unusually anticipated by Echelon late in Act 4 —
    the leash is bidirectional in ways Noelle does not fully model.
- The implant is regime-as-passenger. It rhymes thematically with Aeron's
  Branding scar (regime-as-mark), Lyra's Band (regime-as-constraint), and
  Nyra's silver palm (regime-as-vow). Noelle's body carries the system.
- **Aether Tags are reverse-engineered from Bands**, the same way Bands are
  reverse-engineered from Cores. CAD researchers were testing Band 2.0 on
  themselves. Noelle is, in a literal sense, a piece of the regime's
  research-on-itself.

## 4. THE LIORA FILE

- Noelle has Liora Rylan's CAD file. She has had it since several months
  before her defection.
- Discovery context: during the personal-edit period (when Noelle was
  conducting illicit self-audits), she pulled a batch of redacted civilian
  files at random for variance analysis. Liora's was in the batch.
- **Noelle did not know whose file it was.** She did not know the surname.
  She did not connect "Liora" to any Rylan she had heard of. She read the
  data, recognized something in the pattern she could not articulate, and
  buried the file in her offline cache. She did not flag it. She did not
  report it. She did not know why she was protecting it.
- This is canon's first instance of Noelle's *unmeasurable instinct*
  operating before she has a framework for it. The thing Lyra teaches her
  exists in `a3_int_02_noelle_unmeasurable_emp` — Noelle was already doing
  before she had the category for it.
- The file contains:
  - Liora's CAD-flag history (low NDI, courier-network adjacent, "monitor").
  - A note in CAD's hand: *"do not action without Faber's countersign."*
    Faber never countersigned. Liora was never actioned.
  - Cross-references to a "Verdant cohort" tag. (See §8.)
- Noelle decrypts the Liora file's full meaning during Protocol Verdant
  decoding (see §8). She tells Aeron in the second movement of the
  Verdant payoff scene.
- **Selene knew Liora was alive via Kael's Outlands network. Noelle has had
  Liora's CAD file for months without knowing it was Aeron's mother.**
  Two of the rebellion's strategic minds have been holding pieces of
  Aeron's family without telling him. Noelle's holding was unconscious;
  Selene's was deliberate. The contrast matters.

## 5. THE FIRST NDI SUBJECT

- Noelle's parents, **Anesh and Talen Korr**, are not the "civil
  statisticians in a Harmony-aligned census office" their public records
  describe. They are CAD-adjacent contractors. Their actual project, run
  out of a quiet upper-middle research suite for the better part of two
  decades, was **a longitudinal study on pattern-recognition prodigies.**
- Noelle was the primary subject. She is the foundational subject of the
  Neural Disposition Index. NDI's behavioral baselines — the canonical
  template every other citizen's score is calibrated against — are her
  childhood data.
- **What this means:**
  - Her early "praise for pattern recognition" was data collection.
  - Her academic placement was steered.
  - Her recruitment into CAD was not merit; she was being routed home.
  - When she designed NDI as an adult, she was extending the model that
    had been built around her since age four.
- Her parents are alive. They live in the Aeries upper-middle tier. They
  have not contacted her since her defection. She has not contacted them.
  Each side knows where the other is. The silence is the relationship.
- **Structural rhyme with Marcus:** Marcus turned childhood loss into
  doctrine. The Korrs turned childhood pattern recognition into doctrine.
  Same shape — academic violence rather than military violence. Their
  relationship to Noelle and Marcus's relationship to Aeron are the same
  question asked in different registers: *what does it mean to be the
  child a parent reduced to a use case?*
- Noelle does not realize she was the NDI baseline until late in the
  Defection Coda or Verdant decrypt — she has known her parents were
  CAD-adjacent for some time, but the depth of her own subject status is
  the third layer of revelation in the coda scene.

## 6. THE CHILD

- File designation: **Mira (Subject 7, Cohort C-14).** Age in story window:
  approximately 9 in 383 AC, 16 in 390 AC.
- Mira is one of the children whose harmonic compliance conditioning Noelle
  designed during her CAD years. The conditioning worked. Mira is alive,
  functional, Echelon-loyal, model citizen-coded. She lives in the Mid
  tier with foster placements managed by Harmony Bureau social services.
- **Noelle has the file. She has not been able to bring herself to look up
  where Mira lives.** This sits in her Defection Coda data as the file
  she opens last and closes fastest.
- **Operational presence:** Mira surfaces once, during an Act 4 mission
  involving a Harmony Bureau youth event. Noelle recognizes her own
  conditioning patterns in the child's responses. She does not approach.
  - **EMP:** Noelle tells the team. The team cannot save Mira during this
    op; deconditioning would take months and a stable environment neither
    side can offer. Noelle has to walk away from the child she designed.
    The cost stays with her.
  - **OB:** Noelle treats the recognition as tactical data — "a known
    asset; deployable later." She does not approach Mira. The cost is
    that she does not register the cost.
- **Light touch.** Mira is not a major arc. She is the carry-weight that
  proves Noelle's analytical guilt is operational, not abstract. Do not
  expand Mira into a sub-plot. One file mention in the coda; one
  recognition beat in an Act 4 mission; one closing line in late-game
  reflection. That is the full footprint.

## 7. DIRECTOR FABER (CAD ANTAGONIST)

- **Director Faber.** Single name in scenes — "Director Faber" or just
  "Faber" — never given a first name. Latin *faber* = artisan, maker,
  shaper. Etymological double-meaning includes "fabrication."
- Faber was Noelle's direct supervisor inside CAD. Mid-fifties at story
  start. Still active in the directorate.
- Faber signed the operational order on Halen. Faber authorized the memory
  edits on Noelle. Faber's countersign protocol is what protected Liora's
  file from being actioned. **The same hand that protected Aeron's mother
  killed Noelle's partner.** Faber is not a moustache-twirler — Faber is
  an institutional craftsman who shapes outcomes the way a sculptor shapes
  stone, including the rare unpunished mercy.
- Faber's voice still lives in Noelle's head. Late in her drafting work
  (`a4_s09_emp` and `a4_s10_ob`), Noelle catches herself using Faber's
  cadence. *"The one-pass rule is the instrument I use to prevent the
  doubts from becoming arguments"* (existing OB doctrine line) is a Faber
  formulation. Noelle does not realize it until the coda.
- **Faber does not appear on screen in current scenes.** Faber's name is
  threaded through Noelle's existing dialogue across A2_S08, A3_S19,
  A4_S09 / A4_S10. Three mentions, three different functions: an
  antagonist, a teacher, a ghost. Possible Act 5 confrontation if you want
  one — not authored.
- **The unanswered question Noelle carries:** was Faber edited too? Did
  Faber sign the order on Halen freely, or does Faber's compliance also
  trace back to a CAD recalibration session? Noelle has the data to
  investigate this. She has not. The question is left open in canon.

## 8. PROTOCOL VERDANT

- Seeded in `a2_s08_the_analyst`: Noelle finds Aeron's name in 17
  classified entries tagged "Protocol Verdant." She tells him she will
  keep digging.
- **Protocol Verdant is the regime's bloodline-preservation program.**
  Active for at least 30 years, possibly longer. Mandate: identify, monitor,
  and preserve viable Verdant and Ethereal lineages for future Core
  research applications.
- **What Aeron is, per Protocol Verdant:** the last viable pure Verdant
  candidate identified in the city. He was preserved — his Branding
  rejection was used as evidence of lineage purity, not failure. Marcus's
  cover story of "fate" was the public lie; Protocol Verdant was the
  internal truth. Aeron was being kept clean for breeding — for
  resonance-compatibility experiments scheduled in the late 380s with a
  matched Ethereal subject.
- **The matched Ethereal subject is Lyra.** Lyra's prodigy status,
  academic placement, and proximity to the Rylan family across her
  childhood were all Protocol Verdant placement work. Lyra does not know
  this. She believes her path was Cathedral assignment. The Cathedral
  believes it was Cathedral assignment. The placement was authored by CAD
  three layers up.
- **Noelle decrypts Protocol Verdant in late Act 4** in the Verdant
  Payoff scene (see §11). EMP and OB versions diverge sharply:
  - **EMP:** She tells Aeron what he was preserved as, and tells Lyra
    what she was placed as. She tells them in the same room. The
    revelation is brutal but held. *"You did not love each other because
    you were free. You loved each other in spite of being placed.
    Whatever this is now — that is the part the regime did not author."*
  - **OB:** She tells Aeron alone. She does not tell Lyra. Aeron files
    Lyra-as-placement as tactical data. *"This is something I can use
    against him,"* she says, meaning Marcus. She does not mean Lyra. She
    has stopped distinguishing.
- **The Liora file's "Verdant cohort" tag (§4) clicks here.** Liora is in
  Protocol Verdant's database not as a subject but as **a cataloged escape**
  — the only Verdant-adjacent person on file who left the city undetected
  and was never re-actioned. Noelle's file has been quietly tracking her
  for the regime as a flag-but-do-not-pursue case. Faber's countersign
  protocol is what kept Liora alive in the data without anyone going to
  fetch her.
- The Verdant payoff scene therefore has three movements:
  1. What Aeron was preserved for (and what Lyra was placed as, EMP only).
  2. What it means about Marcus's "fate" lie (it was a lie about a deeper
     truth — Aeron was an investment, not destiny).
  3. The Liora file: she was a tracked escape; Noelle has had her records
     for months; Liora is alive and findable.

## 9. THE DEFECTION (REVISED)

- The existing defection canon (§4.4 of the character profile) remains
  true: stress-related-psychosis cover, data crystal, intentional
  corruption cascade, escape via Courier protection. **What is added:**
- Noelle did not assemble the personal-edit evidence alone. A SAD analyst
  whose name she cannot fully retrieve — possibly *V—n* or *Vehn* — saw
  the editing pattern from a Signal & Analytics angle and routed encrypted
  fragments to her over a period of weeks. They were deleted before
  Noelle could thank them.
- **She holds the rebellion's most damning evidence because someone she
  cannot remember thanking died for it.** This is the whistleblower
  beat folded inside the Defection Coda — single nthought passage, not
  its own scene.
- The Defection Coda (§11.1) stages: (1) the realization of the editing,
  (2) the discovery that the edit was hiding Halen's murder, (3) the
  recognition that her own NDI baseline came from her parents. Three
  layers of revelation, in that order.

## 11. SCENES TO AUTHOR

### 11.1 Defection Coda (`a4_s10b_noelle_coda_emp` / `_ob`)

- **Shape:** Coda riding `a4_s09_noelle_implicated_emp` (EMP) and
  `a4_s10_noelle_doctrine_ob` (OB). Same structural pattern as Kael's
  `a4_s10a` riding the EMP `a4_s10`.
- **Beat:** Noelle is drafting a framework / doctrine. She catches her
  own cadence and recognizes Faber's voice in her writing. She opens her
  defection data crystal — the one she has not opened since the rebellion
  established its base — to remind herself why she defected. She finds
  three things in three minutes:
  1. Her own name on logs she cannot remember authoring. (Existing canon,
     dramatized for the first time.)
  2. *Halen — Outer Wards Resonance, year four of assignment.* Cross-checks
     against dispatch manifests. No Halen ever arrived. The transfer was a
     fiction. Halen died on the day the records say they were reassigned.
  3. The cohort metadata on her own NDI baseline file points to her
     parents' research suite. *KORR_A_TALEN_PROJECT_LEAD.* Anesh and
     Talen Korr are in the byline of her own foundational data.
- **Ending:** She does not cry. She closes the crystal. She returns to the
  framework she was drafting. She types one more line. She deletes it.
  - **EMP:** The deleted line was *"this isn't support, it's editing."*
    The same line she found in her own handwriting before the defection.
    She has just written it again. She does not show Aeron yet.
  - **OB:** The deleted line was *"Faber would approve of this draft."*
    She types it. She deletes it. She rewrites the framework cleaner.
    She does not stop.
- Whistleblower nthought folds into the second beat: *"someone gave me
  this. I cannot remember their face. Their hands gave me the future they
  would not have."*

### 11.3 Tessa–Noelle Anchor (`a3_p18_implant_emp` / `_ob`)

- Mid-Act 3. Tessa is doing routine medical follow-up months after
  Noelle's intake; Noelle has been complaining of low-grade headaches.
  Tessa scans her and finds the Aether Tag at the base of her skull.
- This is the structural follow-up to A2_P14 ("The Patient"). In A2_P14
  Tessa cracked Noelle's framework with the word *care.* Here Noelle is
  the patient with the survival probability.
- **EMP:** Tessa removes the implant. Awake surgery. Noelle holds the
  conversation through the procedure as a coping mechanism. The
  conversation lands on Halen — Noelle has just discovered the death in
  the coda the night before; she has not told anyone; she tells Tessa
  while Tessa is cutting into her skull. *"I do not know if I am crying
  about a person I cannot remember loving or about the procedure. The
  data does not separate cleanly."* Tessa: *"It does not have to. Hold
  still."*
- **OB:** Tessa identifies the implant and recommends removal. Noelle
  refuses. *"It is a closed-loop. We control what they see. Leaving it
  active is operationally superior."* Tessa argues briefly. Noelle holds.
  **Tessa does not walk away.** She says: *"All right. Then I'll watch
  the room."* And she does. For the rest of the act. The friendship is
  not broken. **Noelle is.** What withdraws on OB is not Tessa's
  willingness to give care — Tessa's character is the hand that stays —
  it is Noelle's capacity to receive it. The system performed this edit
  on her once with Halen. The OB version is the edit she is now
  performing on herself.

### 11.4 Civilizational Memory-Editing Thesis (`a3_s20_unwitnessed_emp` / `_ob`)

- Late Act 3, council scene. Selene has called the room. Noelle has been
  asked to brief on Echelon's intelligence apparatus.
- Noelle delivers a thesis the rebellion has never heard articulated
  this clearly: **the war is not over governance, it is over the right
  to remember.** Memory editing makes testimony impossible. Records can
  be falsified before evidence reaches the network. The Purge is
  provable only because Noelle smuggled the data out before the edit
  cycle completed; if the rebellion fights conventionally and slowly, the
  evidence can be gone before they reach an audience.
- **Key line:** *"They do not have to win. They have to outlast our
  capacity to remember why we are fighting. If we lose six months, we
  lose the case. If we lose two years, we lose the cohort that
  remembers. The clock is not military. The clock is forensic."*
- The scene reframes the rebellion's strategic horizon. It is also the
  speech that makes Noelle the strategic spine of the rebellion in a way
  she has not been before. Selene defers to her in the room. This is
  load-bearing for `a4_p15` (Selene × Noelle, "The Cost of Certainty").
- **Path differences are tonal, not structural.** EMP delivery: Noelle
  ends on *"so we have to be witnessed before we are erased."* OB
  delivery: Noelle ends on *"so we have to erase them before they erase
  us."* Same thesis, opposite ethics.

## 13. PATH EXPRESSIONS

### 13.1 EMP Path

- The trauma layer makes EMP Noelle's vulnerability earnable rather than
  declared. She does not become emotionally available because Aeron is
  the right person; she becomes available because the system removed her
  prior reference point and she is rebuilding from scratch. Aeron is the
  first thing she has loved with full memory of loving it.
- The Liora file gift in §11.2 is the most emotionally adult moment in
  her arc. She gives Aeron his mother by way of giving him her months
  of unconscious mercy. He has to receive it.
- The Tessa anchor heals A2_P14 fully. Tessa is now Noelle's medical
  intimate; the alliance has muscle, not just words.

### 13.2 OB Path

- The trauma layer makes OB Noelle's complicity sharper. She is not
  becoming Faber out of weakness — she is becoming Faber because the
  system that produced her never stopped producing her. The Korr family
  line continues through her drafting hand. Faber's countersign protocol
  is what she has unconsciously inherited.
- The Tessa anchor on OB does not break the friendship; it breaks
  Noelle's capacity to receive Tessa. Tessa stays in the room. Noelle
  stops being able to feel her stay. This is the OB cost in the
  friendship register: not the withdrawal of the people who taught her,
  but **the withdrawal of her own ability to be reached by what they
  teach.** It mirrors the Halen edit — what was done to her, she is now
  doing to herself. The implant left active is the door; the Glass
  Moment (§11.6) is the room behind it.
- The implant left active is the OB body horror in canon form. She
  carries the regime in her skull voluntarily because operational
  control is preferable to bodily autonomy. This is the OB thesis in a
  single decision.
