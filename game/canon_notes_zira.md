# CANON NOTES — ZIRA ("ASH-9")

Authoritative reference for the Zira storyline after the integration /
oomph expansion pass. Any scene touching Zira, the Breach, the Choice at
the Console, the Last Transmission, the Nine Names, the Beacon Chip's
cryptographic anchor, the Three Core Nodes, Marna, or the Cracked Wafer
must be consistent with this file. If a scene contradicts this file, the
scene is wrong.

Last updated: 2026-05-05 (Zira integration pass).

---

## 1. CORE FACTS

- **Zira (callsign Ash-9)** — Ghostline architect; Sector 12-B Unders
  origin; age 32 in Act 4 (390 AC), 25 at story start (383 AC). Pre-
  existing canon (`26_Zira_Character_Profile`) remains authoritative for
  psychology, voice, visual profile, and base relationship shapes. This
  document **adds canon**; it does not replace it.
- **Renn Kael remains the spine.** Every addition in this document is
  calibrated to deepen the Renn arc, not compete with it. Where existing
  canon says Renn was executed by Marcus on public feed at Sector 12-B
  during the Breach (378-379 AC), the addition is contextual: *what
  preceded the execution, what was being broadcast at the moment, what
  the broadcast contained, and why Renn was at the transmitter when
  Marcus arrived.*
- **Zira was born in the Unders.** Canon-locked. Not an Aeries defector.
  Ground-up rebellion architecture.
- The Breach happened 378-379 AC. Zira was 20-21. **The override did
  not misfire.** The override worked. The choice at the console is the
  scene's actual moral content (see §2).
- Renn left a single encrypted file on his cracked wafer that Zira has
  been trying to decrypt for **eleven years** as of Act 4. It contains
  his last broadcast (see §3).
- The "9" in Ash-9 names nine specific people Zira knows by name (see §4).
- Ghostline's authentication system is cryptographically keyed on Renn's
  voice (see §5).
- Marna died six months after the Breach (see §7). Faber-signed
  disposition.

## 2. THE CHOICE AT THE CONSOLE

The reframe of the Breach event. Existing canon ("her override
misfired") is replaced by the following:

- The night of the Breach, Echelon's raid was approximately three
  minutes from Sector 12-B's perimeter. Zira had her hands on the
  console with two operations queued and **a single power channel
  shared between them.**
  - Operation A: trigger the **evacuation override.** Pull the four
    youngest operators from the relay floor — Tev (15), Kessa (17),
    Ami (18), Yves (18). The override would have moved them through
    the maintenance shafts to a fallback hide three sectors over.
  - Operation B: hold the **Sector 12-B warning broadcast** active for
    two more minutes. Long enough to push the warning to seventeen
    other Unders cells across the city. Several of those cells had
    Echelon raids inbound on the same hour.
- **She could not do both.** The evacuation override and the broadcast
  amplifier shared the same power channel. Triggering one cut the other.
- **She asked Renn.** Renn was at the transmitter, ten meters from
  Zira's console, with his hand on the broadcast key. He had ten seconds
  to answer.
- **Renn chose the broadcast.** *"Push the warning. Get the others out.
  I'll hold the post."* Standard Renn — the math, the patience, the
  trust. He stayed at the transmitter to maintain signal continuity
  while Zira pushed the broadcast.
- Zira pushed the broadcast. Several of the seventeen warned cells
  evacuated successfully. (Best estimate: forty to sixty lives saved
  across those cells across the next forty-eight hours.)
- Renn stayed at the transmitter. Marcus's team breached during the
  broadcast. **Marcus shot Renn at the transmitter, on feed, mid-cycle.**
- The broadcast continued for forty-two seconds after Renn was dead.
  Renn had configured the repeater chains earlier to maintain signal
  continuity if the operator went down. The continuation was Renn's
  preparation, not an accident.
- The four young operators died at their posts. The five senior
  operators (see §4 for full list) died at theirs. Nine in Sector 12-B.
- **Zira has been carrying for eleven years: *I asked him to choose. He
  chose the broadcast. The broadcast killed him. The nine I could not
  save are the price of saving the seventeen cells. Renn validated the
  math. The math was right. The math is also why he is dead.***
- The grief structure is **not** survivor's guilt of an accident. It
  is **partnership in the decision.** Zira asked. Renn agreed. Both of
  their hands are on the choice. Marcus pulled the trigger; the choice
  put Renn at the trigger.
- Renn's last broadcast (§3) confirms the partnership. Renn's final
  words validate the choice. The validation is the cruelty — if he had
  blamed her, she could refuse the blame. He didn't.

### 2.1 What is unchanged

- Zira's eardrum was ruptured at the console — the override fire damaged
  the relay shielding when the broadcast amplifier maxed out. The
  eardrum rupture remains canon. The cause is now slightly different
  (broadcast overload, not override misfire).
- Marcus shot Renn on public feed. The execution is unchanged. **Marcus
  did not know the broadcast was Renn's choice; Marcus assumed Renn was
  the senior operator who refused to evacuate.** Marcus's act remains
  Marcus's act. The reframe does not redirect blame.
- Zira found Renn's cracked wafer under debris after the raid. Unchanged.
- Ghostline-Theta was rebuilt from the wafer. Unchanged.
- Renn's 37-second voice recording (relay junction reset) is preserved
  on the three core nodes. Unchanged.

### 2.2 Structural rhyme

- This is the second time in the project's canon that a Rylan-adjacent
  death reframes from "victim of regime" to "co-author of the moment of
  death." The first is Halen Vance (Noelle's pass) — Halen made the call
  that ended their relationship's safety. Renn made the call that ended
  his life.
- The rhyme is intentional. It establishes that the rebellion's deepest
  griefs are not done *to* its members; they are made *by* its members
  in moments where the alternative was unbearable.
- Zira and Noelle now share the shape of their wounds: both are
  survivors of *partnerships in cost.* Zira asked Renn to choose. Halen
  was edited from Noelle. Different mechanisms; same inheritance.

## 3. THE LAST TRANSMISSION

- Renn's cracked wafer holds **one encrypted file Zira has been trying
  to decrypt for eleven years.** The encryption is not Echelon's. It is
  Renn's personal cipher — devised between him and Zira when she was
  twelve, during a winter when they were repairing relay shielding and
  needed something private to talk through that no Echelon scanner
  could read. It has never been used for operational traffic. It was
  for them.
- **Zira has been hunting fragments of the cipher's missing key for
  eleven years.** Every Ghostline maintenance cycle, she runs the
  encrypted block against whatever new fragment she has acquired. The
  ritual happens nightly. She has performed it approximately four
  thousand times. Nothing has unlocked it.
- The fragments accumulate from intercepted CAD signals, salvaged
  Echelon traffic, archive raids, and operator-to-operator handoffs.
  Each cycle gives her at most a single byte of progress, more often
  none. The cipher's key length is in the hundreds of bytes. The
  patience is the work.
- **The final fragment arrives via a routine Ghostline pull that
  happens to land on a CAD-internal traffic burst.** Pure operational
  luck after eleven years of patience. She gets it not because anyone
  gave it to her but because she never stopped pulling. The audience
  learns this in scene: the decryption tonight is the *thousandth time
  this month* she has tried, on top of eleven years of trying. The
  patience is the character.
- **The decrypted file is Renn's last broadcast** — the thing he was
  transmitting when Marcus shot him.
- The broadcast is not heroic. Not addressed to Zira. Not a final
  declaration. **The broadcast is a working session.** Calm, technical,
  exactly the voice of the 37-second recording. Renn explains, to
  whoever finds this, how to keep Ghostline alive if he doesn't make
  it home that night. The successor instructions. Patient. Practical.
- The broadcast runs approximately five minutes and twelve seconds in
  audio. Most of it is technical: relay junction protocols, repeater
  chain configuration, beacon credential rotation procedures, the
  geographic distribution he wanted the first ten Ghostline nodes to
  follow.
- **The final block is short.** Renn becomes aware of the breach
  proximity. His voice does not change pitch. He says (do not paraphrase):

> *"Z — I'm hearing them at the perimeter. Two minutes maybe. I'm
> going to hold the post until the broadcast hits the seventeenth
> cell. After that, the repeater chain takes over. I configured it
> last week. You didn't know. Sorry — wanted you to have a
> redundancy you didn't owe me.*
> 
> *You asked. I answered. I'd answer the same. Keep the line
> alive. Out."*

- **Out.** The last word is the standard radio sign-off. The cruelest
  word in the broadcast, because it is the last word.
- **He calls her *Z* and *kid.*** Not Zero — Zero is not canon; it was
  author-process trivia from the early concept days. Renn's
  vocabulary for Zira is *Z* (operational) and *kid* (familial — used
  earlier in the broadcast's technical section but not in the final
  block).
- **The compression is the load.** The five lines in the final block
  carry the entire weight: *You asked* names the partnership;
  *I answered* names his agency; *I'd answer the same* validates the
  choice (his version of *I love you* in his actual register —
  technical, compressed, exact); *Keep the line alive* is both the
  operational instruction and the connection to him; *Out* is the
  sign-off. Renn does not get to the larger conversation Zira has
  been imagining for eleven years. He gets to five sentences. The
  broadcast is shorter than her imagined version. **The not-getting-
  to-it is itself the cost.**
- The broadcast cuts out at *Out.* No gunshot in the recording — Marcus
  shot Renn approximately four seconds after Renn signed off. The four
  seconds are the silence Zira hears at the end of the file.
- **Zira hears the broadcast for the first time in eleven years on the
  decryption night.** This is the EMP/OB scene split (see §11).

## 4. THE NINE NAMES

The "9" in Ash-9 names nine specific Sector 12-B operators who died in
the Breach. **Their names are the cryptographic salt for Ghostline's
authentication system.** The network's packet-validation protocol
cycles through the nine names as part of its hash routine. **Ghostline
cannot validate a packet without transmitting the names.** Every burst
transmission across the network's eleven-year operational history has
carried the nine names in a sub-aural cryptographic layer. **Zira built
remembrance into the protocol. She made grief operationally mandatory.
She did it to herself. She did not tell anyone.**

### 4.1 The nine names and roles

**The four youngest (the evacuation candidates Renn chose not to pull):**

| Name | Age | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Tev** | 15 | Youngest. Workbench apprentice; was learning relay assembly from Renn. Talked to machines while he worked, like Renn. |
| **Kessa** | 17 | Renn's broadcast apprentice. At the transmitter post with Renn at the moment of choice. Marcus shot Kessa first; Renn second; the bodies were three meters apart. |
| **Ami** | 18 | Marna's stall apprentice. Was running relay overflow that night to cover two operators down with fever. |
| **Yves** | 18 | Renn's relay protégé. Could solo a junction reset by sixteen. Renn's letter (§3) "the four kids" specifically refers to these four. |

**The five senior operators (would have died regardless of override choice):**

| Name | Age | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Sera Vex** | 26 | Relay coordinator. **Kira's mother.** Was at the floor's coordination console. (Sera = canon-locked from existing 26_Zira profile; the additions here lock her age and role.) |
| **Linn Vex** | 28 | Sera's partner. **Kira's other parent.** Supply/courier interface. Did not survive the night. **Kira lost both parents at Sector 12-B.** |
| **Halix** | 24 | Ghostrunner; route-finder. Mapped the original Sector 12-B Ghostline node distribution before the rebuild. Halix's routing notes survive on Node Corvid (§8). |
| **Doss** | 32 | Senior courier. Knew every smuggler line into the Wards. Renn coordinated with Doss for the seventeen-cell warning routing. |
| **Brann** | 53 | Oldest. Senior technician. Trained Renn. Trained Sera. Trained the four young. Was the relay's institutional memory. |

### 4.2 Why nine and not eight, ten, twenty

- The five senior operators died at their posts during Marcus's raid.
  Standard rebellion-cell collapse pattern. The five would have died
  whether Zira pushed the broadcast or the evacuation. Renn's choice
  did not affect their fates.
- The four young operators *would* have been pulled out by the
  evacuation override. They died because the override fired the
  broadcast amplifier instead. Renn's choice killed them.
- Zira does not differentiate. She carries all nine. The math she
  cannot stop running: *if I had pushed the override instead, four of
  them would have lived. If I had pushed neither, the seventeen cells
  would have been hit unwarned. The choice was for the four against
  the seventeen-cells'-worth of lives. The math was right. The math
  killed four people I knew by name.*

### 4.3 The cryptographic salt mechanic

- Ghostline-Theta's authentication system uses a hash function whose
  salt is the concatenation of the nine names in chronological order
  of death (Tev, Kessa, Ami, Yves, Sera, Linn, Halix, Doss, Brann).
- Every packet sent across Ghostline includes a hash signature
  computed from the salt + payload + timestamp + sender credential.
- Validating a received packet requires re-computing the hash, which
  requires the salt, which requires the nine names. The names are
  embedded in every Ghostrunner's beacon token at issuance.
- **Every Ghostrunner has been carrying the nine names since
  credentialing without knowing it.** The names are the network's
  ground truth. The network cannot operate without them.
- Aeron's beacon credential (the chip from the Bridge — see §5) carries
  the salt. He has been authenticating against the names for the
  duration of his Ghostline access.
- The reveal: in scene (see §11), Aeron sees a string of names scrolling
  under the carrier hiss layer during a Ghostline diagnostic. He
  recognizes one — possibly Sera, possibly Tev, possibly Brann. He
  asks. Zira names them. All nine. To him. For the first time aloud.

### 4.4 What this does to the character

- Ghostline is, at its lowest layer, a memorial. Zira built the
  rebellion's most operationally important communications network as
  a way to keep transmitting the nine. The transmission is automatic.
  She does not have to think about it. The transmission happens four
  hundred thousand times a day across the network. **Every burst is a
  recitation.**
- She did not tell the rebellion. The rebellion has been running on
  her grief for eleven years and nobody knew. Selene did not know.
  Aeron did not know. Lyra did not know.
- The decision to use the nine as salt was not formal. It was the
  cryptographic shorthand she reached for when she was rebuilding the
  network in the months after the Breach. The names were the only
  string she could remember in order without writing them down. They
  became the salt because they were what was in her head when the
  protocol needed a salt.
- She has not told Aeron about the salt by Act 4 default. The Nine
  Names beat (§11.4 / threading) is where it surfaces.

## 5. THE BEACON CHIP CARRIES RENN

- Ghostline's authentication system is **cryptographically keyed on
  Renn's voice.** Specifically, the 37-second relay-junction-reset
  recording (the one preserved on the three core nodes — see §8) is
  the frequency-domain anchor for the network's credential validation.
- Every beacon chip Zira has ever issued contains a frequency-matched
  fragment of Renn's voice as part of its credential signature.
  Validating a credential requires the receiving node to compare the
  presented fragment against the canonical recording.
- **The Beacon Chip Zira gave Aeron at Obsidian Bridge carries Renn's
  voice as its signature.** Aeron has been authenticating against
  Renn's voice every time he has used Ghostline since Act 1.
- Aeron does not know. The signature mechanic is operationally
  invisible — the comparison happens at hardware level, not in the
  user-facing protocol.
- **Zira gave Aeron her dead brother's voice as his credential.** That
  is what the Beacon Chip is. The most sacred artifact she has, given
  to a Glass-coded Echelon officer at a bridge in the dark, on the
  evidence of one mercy choice. The audience learns the weight of the
  gift only when Zira tells Aeron in scene.

### 5.1 The OB-path implication

- **On OB, Aeron rejected the Beacon Chip at Obsidian Bridge.** Per
  existing canon: at the bridge, mercy_total below threshold leaves the
  chip undelivered. OB Aeron has been operating on Ghostline without a
  beacon credential the entire time.
- **Zira has been manually overriding the credential check for OB
  Aeron's transmissions across Acts 2-4.** Every Ghostline-mediated
  scene with OB Aeron retroactively becomes Zira choosing to vouch for
  him by hand rather than letting him authenticate against her brother.
- The vouching is its own grief. **OB Aeron has not been allowed to
  hear Renn's voice as his credential because he did not earn it at
  the bridge.** Zira has been holding Renn back from him. The holding
  is consistent with Zira's OB trajectory — the closer Aeron gets to
  Marcus's shape, the further Zira holds Renn from him.
- **OB Aeron will never hear the broadcast in §3.** The Last
  Transmission scene's OB version (see §11) is Zira alone. She will not
  give him Renn even after eleven years of waiting to hear it herself.
- This recontextualizes the OB path's overall Aeron-Zira dynamic: she
  has been the one holding the line on what he is allowed to receive
  from her, and the line has been her brother. She has been protecting
  Renn from Aeron's drift the whole time.

### 5.2 The EMP-path payoff

- **EMP Aeron accepted the Beacon Chip at the bridge.** He has been
  carrying Renn's voice as credential since Act 1.
- When Zira tells him in The Last Transmission scene, every prior
  Ghostline scene gets retroactive weight. The trust has been
  literal: she gave him her brother to authenticate against, and he
  has not betrayed it.
- This is the kind of revelation that does not require dramatization
  beyond the disclosure line. The weight is in what Aeron now
  understands has been true for the entire run of the story.

## 6. THE CRACKED WAFER

Existing canon establishes Zira found Renn's cracked wafer under debris
after the Breach and rebuilt Ghostline-Theta from it. The wafer
remains canon. **What it contains, locked:**

- **Partial Sector 12-B network topology** (the original 79-node mesh
  before the Breach reduced viable infrastructure to 37 rebuildable
  nodes). Used by Zira as the geographic skeleton for Ghostline's
  rebuild.
- **Routing algorithms and repeater chain configurations.** The
  technical layer Renn's career was built on. Zira has been using and
  refining these for eleven years.
- **Corrupted operator logs** spanning approximately eighteen months
  of pre-Breach Sector 12-B activity. Recoverable in fragments;
  partial reads have given Zira context about the operators who died.
  Three of the nine names came back to Zira via these logs, not from
  her own memory.
- **Renn's 37-second voice recording.** The relay junction reset.
  Preserved in three canonical copies on the three core nodes (see §8).
- **The single encrypted file — the Last Transmission (§3).** Sealed
  with Renn's personal cipher. Zira has been working on it for eleven
  years.

The wafer is in Zira's workshop, in a small lead-lined drawer beneath
the primary workstation. She has not moved it since 379 AC. She has
never let anyone else handle it. Aeron has seen the drawer; he has not
seen the wafer.

## 7. MARNA — THE MOTHER

- **Marna** (no surname canonized; the Vex / Kael / unspecified
  patterning of Sector 12-B family naming is loose). Zira's mother.
  Pre-Breach: ran a parts stall by day, repaired relay conduits by
  night. Existing canon-locked.
- **Marna died approximately six months after the Breach** —
  approximately mid-380 AC.
- **Cause: Cognitive Architecture Directorate intervention.** Listed
  in CAD records as voluntary wellness termination after Marna refused
  to stop working on the relay benches. **Faber's signature on the
  disposition order.** The same hand that signed Halen's transfer
  fiction (Noelle pass) and Liora's protective protocol (Verdant
  decrypt).
- Marna was on a watch list because she was Renn's mother. Echelon's
  post-Breach cleanup included extended monitoring of Sector 12-B
  family connections. Marna's continued relay-bench work registered
  as "non-compliance" within ninety days. The wellness intervention
  was scheduled, performed, logged, closed.
- Zira was 21. She was rebuilding Ghostline. Marna's death registered
  with her as: *the regime took the brother on a public feed and the
  mother in a clinical room, and both were Faber's paperwork.*
- Marna's death is **not** Zira's fault, the way the Breach's nine are
  the math she carries. Marna is grief without choice, the loss that
  arrived without a console-question. Zira mentions Marna rarely.
- **Marna does not appear in The Last Transmission.** Renn's
  compressed final block does not get to Marna — he runs out of
  time. The conversation Zira has been imagining for eleven years
  was longer than the actual broadcast. The not-getting-to-Marna
  is its own grief: the things Renn would have said if he had
  more than five sentences are forever unsaid. Marna's fate stays
  canonical via the Faber-signed disposition; she is just not
  named in the recording.
- Marna's fate is canonical and threadable. Future scenes referencing
  Zira's family history should respect the Faber-disposition framing.

## 8. THE THREE CORE NODES

The three Ghostline-Theta nodes that hold Renn's 37-second voice
recording in canonical copies. Zira chose their locations. The names
are her own.

- **Node Corvid** — at the rooftop of an abandoned bird-feeder factory
  on Sector 12-B's edge, where Renn taught Zira to listen for crows
  when the relays were down. The crows were the cue to clear the
  band. The factory's loft has been Zira's solo-listening spot since
  she was eleven. Halix's pre-Breach routing notes survive here.
- **Node Solder** — beneath Marna's old parts stall in the Sector
  12-B market collapse. Marna's workbench is the physical anchor. The
  bench was salvaged after the market collapsed and is now part of
  the node's structural support. Zira touches the bench every time
  she services the node.
- **Node Marrow** — at an unmarked junction in the lower Drop where
  the Sector 12-B warning broadcast was last received (its terminal
  hop before the chain repeater took over). The deepest node. Named
  for what bone holds when bone is all that's left. Zira chose the
  name without knowing the broadcast had ended at this junction —
  she just knew the place mattered.
- The three nodes hold the recording redundantly. Loss of any one
  triggers automatic re-replication from the other two. Loss of two
  is the Ghostline-Theta crisis state. **Loss of all three is the
  network's hard failure mode** — Zira has not designed a recovery
  procedure for that scenario because she does not believe she would
  survive it.

## 9. THE PRESSED FLOWER — EXTENDED CANON

Existing canon (per a3_s19b_zira_before_the_weight_ob): Selene gave
Zira a pressed winterbloom, pre-displacement. From before. Zira keeps
it folded inside an oilskin in the workshop. She does not touch it.

**Extended:**

- The flower stays in the oilskin from receipt through The Last
  Transmission scene.
- **Zira touches it for the first time in The Last Transmission
  scene.** After the broadcast plays. After Renn's *Out.* The touch
  is the only physical movement in the scene's final beat. She does
  not pick it up — she rests her hand on it.
- **EMP**: After the touch, she keeps the flower on the bench, not in
  the oilskin. The drawer stays open. Aeron sees it stay there.
- **OB**: After the touch, she returns it to the oilskin and seals
  the drawer. The flower is still hers. The keeping is internal.

## 10. *OUT* AS ZIRA'S SIGN-OFF

After The Last Transmission, **Out** becomes Zira's quiet personal
sign-off in Ghostline traffic. Standard radio sign-off vocabulary, but
its arrival in her vocabulary is canonical. Single word. Audible to
other Ghostrunners as her signature. Future Zira scenes where she
closes a transmission can use it. The word is now hers because Renn
left it to her.

**The word does not propagate to OB.** OB Zira keeps Renn private.
She does not adopt his sign-off because adopting it would be a public
acknowledgment of the broadcast she did not share. OB Zira's sign-off
remains operational-standard.

## 11. PATH EXPRESSIONS

### 11.1 EMP path

- The Choice at the Console becomes communicable on EMP. Zira tells
  Aeron — during The Last Transmission scene — what happened that
  night. The disclosure is the first time she has spoken the choice
  aloud to anyone since Renn died.
- She brings Aeron to her workshop after the decryption completes.
  She plays the broadcast for him. They listen together. She names
  the choice. She names the nine. She touches the flower.
- The broadcast's final line — *I love you. Out.* — lands with Aeron
  in the room. Aeron does not say anything. There is nothing to say.
- The Beacon Chip's secret (carrying Renn) is disclosed in this scene.
  Aeron retroactively understands what he has been carrying for over
  a year.
- *Out* becomes Zira's sign-off. The word is hers from this scene
  forward.
- **EMP Zira is not healed by the disclosure.** She is *witnessed* by
  it. The grief is not lighter. The carrying is no longer alone.

### 11.2 OB path

- The Choice at the Console remains private on OB. Zira does not tell
  Aeron. OB Aeron has been moving toward Marcus's shape; she has
  been holding Renn back from him; the disclosure would close a
  distance she is not willing to close.
- She decrypts alone in her workshop. She plays the broadcast once.
  She files it.
- The broadcast's final line lands on her alone. Renn's *I love you.
  Out.* enters a room that contains only Zira. She does not respond
  aloud. She files it.
- The Beacon Chip's secret is **not** disclosed on OB. OB Aeron does
  not have the chip. He cannot earn the disclosure now. The
  withholding is canonical.
- The pressed flower returns to the oilskin. The drawer seals.
- Zira returns to operational work within ninety minutes. The grief
  stays internal. The work stays the work.
- **OB Zira keeps Renn from the version of Aeron who has become his
  father's shape.** The keeping is the OB cost in the friendship
  register — not a withdrawal of love, but a withdrawal of *what
  she will share with the man Aeron is becoming.* It mirrors the OB
  Tessa-Noelle dynamic structurally: the staying is internal, the
  capacity-to-receive has narrowed.

### 11.3 The Nine Names beat

A small threaded scene where Aeron sees the names scrolling under
Ghostline's carrier hiss layer during a routine diagnostic. He
recognizes one name — Sera, because Kira mentioned her once — and
asks. Zira names all nine. The naming is the rebellion's first audit
of Sector 12-B's specific dead.

- **EMP placement:** In The Last Transmission scene or just before
  it. Author at discretion which.
- **OB placement:** Does not happen as a disclosure scene. The names
  remain in the salt; Aeron does not learn them. The reveal can
  happen *to the audience* via narration in a tactical scene where
  Aeron sees the salt scrolling and is told, off-screen by Zira via
  Ghostline, that "the salt is the salt." He files the answer
  without pressing.

### 11.4 Sera and Kira

- Kira (Zira's orphan ward, Act 2+) is the daughter of Sera Vex and
  Linn Vex. **Kira lost both parents in the Breach.** Both names are
  in the salt.
- Zira has been raising the orphan of two of her named dead. The
  protection of Kira is amends.
- Future Kira scenes should respect this canon. When Kira mentions
  her mother, the audience now knows the mother is salt-encrypted
  into the network Kira has been growing up inside.
