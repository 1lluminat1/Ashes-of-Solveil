# 25_Soren_Rylan_Character_Profile[CANON]

**Born:** 379 AC, Phoenix Secondary Base medical wing

**Current age:** 11 in 390 AC (Act 4) / 12 in 391 AC (Act 5)

**Relatives:** Kael Rylan (father, dec. 381 AC Operation Stillwater), Rava Rylan (mother, dec. 383 AC Operation 391), Aeron Rylan (uncle), Liora Rylan (grandmother, dec. Act 3), Marcus Rylan (paternal grandfather, does not know she exists)

**Location:** Outlands, under the care of Kess Marrow, former apprentice to the late Liora Rylan

## 1. The first two years

Soren was born in Phoenix, named by her father, and raised in the corner of a rebellion by parents who had chosen each other knowing neither of them could promise safety. Her first two years were safe in the way Phoenix-internal children can be safe — which is to say, protected by a community that had made her protection a standing order without telling her it was a standing order. She had her father for two years. She does not remember him. She has been told stories for seven more.

## 2. Her name

Kael chose the name. **Soren** is the feminized form of the Old Tongue word *Seren* — blessing, mercy, the thing the priests of the old Cathedral said over a child when they did not yet know what the child would need protecting from. Kael was not religious. He chose the name anyway. He chose it because his daughter needed the word, and because his mother Liora would recognize it when she heard it.

The name carries a narrative connection across the cast. Lyra Althea, the one living character in the story who speaks Old Tongue at cleric register, will recognize the name immediately as sacred when she meets Soren. That recognition will arrive without being explained. It is one of the things the cast will learn about each other without any of them saying it aloud.

## 3. Stillwater and after

When Kael died at Stillwater in 381 AC, Rava made a decision Selene did not expect and Phoenix did not catch. She took Soren out of Phoenix territory in the immediate aftermath, trusting no one in the rebellion because the rebellion had just lost the person it had been building around, and any post-Stillwater compromise could expose them. She routed herself and Soren through a single low-level courier she had used once before and disappeared into the Unders, settling in Sector 10. Selene, wounded and commanding the Stillwater recovery, looked for them and found nothing. Selene grieved the whole family. The whole family was not yet dead.

For two years, Rava and Soren lived quietly in a Sector 10 tenement. Rava took work in a laundry co-op. She raised Soren on a teaching her dead husband had given her about survival: *some soldiers still got hearts. Your father said there was one kind of soldier who was fine when he was quiet. You ask them if they are taking people. You watch what they say.* Soren carried the teaching abstractly. She did not know that the *"one kind of soldier"* in the teaching was a specific person. She did not know that the test had been written by her father about his little brother. She just knew that her mother's survival teaching said *some soldiers are fine when they are quiet, and here is how to check.*

## 4. The Lower Spans encounter

Early 383 AC, a few days before the Sector 10 sweep. Soren was four. She was with her mother at a water-collection point in the Lower Spans when she slipped between the pipes and encountered an Echelon officer on foot reconnaissance. She ran her mother's test on him. She performed the protective mask first — *"you're one of them"* — and then offered the probe: *"Echelon. The ones that took my dad."* She delivered the inherited line: *"My dad said some soldiers still got hearts."* She watched what he did.

**On EMP paths**, the officer crouched. He admitted he did not know why he was there. When she asked if he was going to take people, he said *"I hope not."* She nodded and said *"Okay"* and disappeared back between the pipes. Her mother, watching from fifteen feet away, had already recognized the officer by family resemblance and was paralyzed. Rava pulled Soren back and walked her home. That night, folding laundry, Rava told her: *"The soldier you talked to in the pipes — I think that was your uncle. Your father's brother. I think his name is Aeron."* That was the first time Soren heard the word *uncle* applied to a specific person.

**On OB paths**, the officer walked past without speaking. Soren said *"Yeah. That's what I thought."* Her mother, watching, identified the officer the same way, was paralyzed the same way, pulled Soren back the same way. That night, folding laundry, Rava told her the same sentence and Soren stored the same word. But Soren's own experience of the soldier had been a cold one. She heard the word *uncle* attached to a cold man.

Neither of them had the morning after the day after that. Operation 391 came.

## 5. The basket and the extraction

When the sweep began, Rava hid Soren in a basket of folded laundry in the back room of the tenement and told her to be quiet. She said: *"You'll be fine if you're quiet. Your uncle was fine when he was quiet."* This was the second time Rava used the word *uncle* aloud in front of Soren, and this time Rava was fully aware of which uncle she meant.

Rava was killed in the sweep. Soren was still in the basket when the Echelon squad left. Within twenty minutes, a courier from Liora Rylan's Outlands network — who had been watching Sector 10 for two years on standing order — entered the tenement, extracted Soren from the basket, and moved her through the courier routes to the Outlands. Soren arrived at Liora's household in 383 AC. She was four.

## 6. Seven years with Liora

Liora raised her as her own granddaughter in every functional sense. She told her about her father continuously from the age of four — not as grief, as inheritance. *"Your father was a man who walked away from the Aeries because the Aeries was not a life he could carry, and the rebellion is what he made instead of the life he could not have. Your name is his blessing on you before he knew what you would need protection from."*

Liora told her about her mother at the age she could hear it. When Soren was nine, Liora showed her the courier report that confirmed what Rava had already said in the tenement: the Echelon officer in the Lower Spans on reconnaissance the day before Operation 391 was Captain Aeron Rylan, Echelon service, officer of record for the sweep that killed her mother. Two independent confirmations, matching. Liora did not tell Soren what to do with the information. She said: *"Your mother told you. The courier also saw. The soldier you met was your uncle Aeron Rylan. I will not tell you what to do with this. When you see him, you will decide."*

At age eleven, Soren knows:

- Her father's full name, his rooftop, his nine years with Selene, his death at Stillwater
- Her mother's full name, the Sector 10 sweep, the basket, the word *uncle*
- The Lower Spans encounter, as a sensory memory — pipes, cold, a soldier's face she has matched to her uncle's name through her mother's identification and Liora's cross-check
- Her uncle Aeron Rylan, as the man who passed or failed her mother's test depending on what happened between the pipes
- Her grandmother Liora, until early 390 AC when Liora was captured and executed
- Kess Marrow, her grandmother's apprentice, now her caretaker

She does not know:

- Whether her uncle, when she sees him again, will be the kind of soldier her mother said her father said existed
- Whether he remembers her

## 7. The Act 5 meeting

When Aeron arrives in the Outlands, Soren recognizes him on sight — the face-match to her memory is immediate, because she has been carrying the memory for seven years with Liora polishing its edges. She does not show the recognition. She watches him.

Across the scene, she decides whether he is the soldier her mother said he was. The decision rests on his behavior with her, not on any single question she asks him. If he earns her trust through the Act 5 meeting's dialogue choices, she tells him the story of the test in her own words. She describes the pipes, the cold, her mother, the test. She names her mother aloud. She tells him that her mother recognized him by the Rylan bones and pulled her back from the pipes. She tells him that her mother called him by his first name that night in their kitchen.

She ends with the question: *"Do you remember a child between pipes?"*

Aeron does the work of translating her sensory memory to his administrative memory. On both EMP and OB paths he has the sensory memory (the pipes, the dirty knuckles, the child). **What differs is what he did.** If he took the reassure branch in `a1_s20_lower_spans`, he tells her he crouched, said he was tired of looking mean, said he hoped he wouldn't take people. If he took the ignore branch, he tells her he walked past without answering. His answer determines whether Soren opens the second stage of the reveal — the basket, the instruction to be quiet, the second use of *uncle*, and the name of the officer who led the operation.

## 8. Path expression

**EMP path:** The meeting is family reconstitution. If Aeron reassured in `a1_s20` and plays the Act 5 dialogue correctly, Soren gives him both stages of the reveal. Aeron leaves the meeting with a niece, a memory of his brother's wife, a name for the child he unknowingly killed, and the knowledge that his one moment of softness in the Lower Spans had been the verdict his sister-in-law was using to decide whether to trust him. The scene is grievable in both directions: Soren has met her uncle, Aeron has met his blood. Both wounds are shared.

**OB path (break-path scene, Option β):** Liora is alive on OB. She grants Aeron one narrow meeting with Soren under specific terms: alone, unarmed, no record, one hour. The same dialogue mechanic applies, but the threshold is higher because OB Aeron has been walking a path that generally precludes the kind of attention Soren is testing for. If he passes, the meeting is the single place in late OB where his path could still bend. If he fails — most OB players will — Soren does not give him the full reveal, he leaves knowing he has a niece but not knowing he killed her mother, and his own subsequent Echelon investigation reconstructs the Rava detail in a late-act-5 private scene. **On OB, the knowledge arrives alone and late, without anyone in the room to share it with.** That is the OB cost: the wound without the grievability.
