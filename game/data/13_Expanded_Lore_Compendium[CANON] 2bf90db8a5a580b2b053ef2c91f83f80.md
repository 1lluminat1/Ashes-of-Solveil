## 1) Canon Clarifications

### 1.1 Governance: No Hidden AI

- **Echelon is entirely human-run.** There is **no AI overlord, sentient network, or algorithmic government.** Any prior references to hidden machine rulers, NAIC-style system control, or “sentient governance” are deprecated and must be removed in all docs and scripts.
- The regime’s brutality, surveillance, and efficiency are the result of:
    - Institutional doctrine.
    - Human decision-making and bureaucracy.
    - Weaponized infrastructure (Harmony Cores, Resonance protocols), but never sentient control.

**Rule:** If a passage implies “the system itself wants X,” rewrite it as “Echelon’s doctrine / leadership / divisions want X.”

---

### 1.2 Harmony Cores: Resonance Relics, Not Alien Tech

- **Harmony Cores are not alien artifacts or batteries.**
- They are fossilized **resonance remnants** from a pre-Collapse age when Verdant and Ethereal lineages were in true alignment. That coherence shattered; the residue is what modern Solveil calls “Cores.”
- Modern Echelon treats Cores as:
    - Power sources.
    - Focus stones for weapons or infrastructure.
    - Testbeds for Resonance conditioning.

This use is a **misinterpretation**. The further human use drifts from harmony principles, the more unstable and dangerous Cores become.

## 2) Core Lore Entries (OB/EMP-Sensitive)

Each entry below is a **single source of truth**. Codex slugs are given for UI integration.

### 2.1 Ghostline

**Codex ID:** `ghostline`

**Type:** Underground communications lattice

**Overview**

Ghostline is a distributed mesh of encrypted relay nodes threaded through the Unders, abandoned conduits, and forgotten maintenance channels. It rides the city’s own noise floor: industrial hum, maintenance pings, calibration tones.

**Ownership & Culture**

- Curated and extended by **Zira (“Ash-9”)** and a loose trust-network of runners and techs.
- Many signals parasitize official maintenance bands, indistinguishable from background calibration noise to Echelon’s filters.
- Ghostrunners tap three times before sending important packets – the Unders’ version of a prayer or oath.

**Function**

- Provides **low-observable burst transmissions** (“ghost hops”) that slip under standard surveillance.
- Used to synchronize rebellion cells, route intel, and echo stolen data across sectors.

**Access**

- Requires a **keyed beacon token** anchored to Zira’s rotating seeds.
- Initial handshakes are **one-way and cautious**; full duplex access only arrives after trust and competence are proven.

**Narrative Hooks**

- EMP route: earlier Ghostline access as trust forms quickly.
- OB route: access is delayed until Aeron proves reliability on a high-risk op.

---

### 2.2 Beacon Chip (Zira’s Chip)

**Codex ID:** `beacon_chip`

**Type:** Cryptographic token / identity beacon

**Overview**

- Physically resembles a dull data wafer.
- **Crucial canon:** the chip **does not** store a readable dossier or “secret file.”
- Instead, it broadcasts a **credential signature** which Ghostline nodes can detect and validate.

**States Across the Story**

1. **Dormant (Act I)**
    - Given to Aeron at Obsidian Bridge.
    - Symbolic mark: flags him to Ghostline as a *potential ally* but remains silent.
    - Represents possibility, not proof.
2. **Activated (Act II)**
    - Once Ghostline sync is established, the chip authenticates Aeron and emits a signed attest: **partial proof** that Marcus authorized the Purge of Sectors 8–10.
    - It never becomes a full, tidy “Purge file;” it’s a fragment strong enough to force moral confrontation.

**Narrative Role**

- Act I: a **silent promise of truth** and a test of Aeron’s empathy at the Bridge (accept or reject).
- Act II: the **connective key** between Aeron, Ghostline, and proof of Marcus’s brutality.

---

### 2.3 Obsidian Bridge

**Codex ID:** `obsidian_bridge`

**Type:** Decommissioned transit span (Sector 10)

**Traits**

- Basalt-black composite structure suspended over industrial void.
- Constant wind shear creates a **stable hiss band** Zira uses as natural carrier noise to mask Ghostline traffic.

**Function in Story**

- Zira selects Obsidian Bridge as the **neutral, risky meeting ground** for her first face-to-face with Aeron.
- The **path lock computation** is anchored here:
    - The chip decision + accumulated path pressure determine whether the story crystallizes as EMP or OB going forward.

**Thematic Role**

- Liminal space between city and void; between obedience and compassion.
- Visually: silhouette, backlight, wind static, and Ghostline’s faint pulse.

---

### 2.4 Purge (Sectors 8–10)

**Codex ID:** `purge_sectors_8_10`

**Type:** State action / atrocity

**Definition**

- Coordinated eradication sweep across Unders Sectors 8–10, publicly framed as “containment” of a fabricated threat.
- Executed with **no pre-announcement**, targeting both inhabitants and infrastructure.

**Canon Constraints**

- Aeron and Lyra **do not know** about the Purge beforehand. Any foreshadowing must be via vague tension or Zira’s hints, never explicit knowledge.
- The Purge cinematic is a **mandatory anchor scene** in Act I and always fires, regardless of path.

**Narrative Function**

- Acts as the **moral breaking point** for Aeron and Lyra:
    - The moment where Echelon’s rhetoric (“order as mercy”) becomes impossible to reconcile with reality.
    - The emotional ground zero for both EMP rebellion and OB denial/guilt.

### 2.5 Harmony Cores (Reframed)

**Codex ID:** `harmony_cores`

**Type:** Resonance relics

**Origin**

- Cohesive artifacts of ancient **Verdant/Ethereal unity** that calcified during a catastrophic resonance collapse. They are **congealed harmony**, not alien machinery.

**Modern Misuse**

- Echelon exploits Cores as:
    - Energy sources.
    - Foci for weapons and experimental Resonance conditioning.
- The deeper their use strays from relational harmony, the more volatility and unintended side effects emerge (structural failure, psychic strain, unpredictable resonance events).

**Design Notes**

- Visual: stone-like, with slow inner flicker rather than sci-fi glow.
- Thematically: they embody the **possibility of balance** and the **danger of power divorced from relationship**.

### 2.6 Verdant & Ethereal Lineages

**Codex ID:** `verdant_ethereal_lineages`

**Type:** Bloodline metaphysics (soft magic)

**Verdant**

- Attuned to **living systems**: growth, ecosystems, somatic empathy.
- Expresses as intuitive understanding of bodies, environments, and subtle interconnected patterns.
- Aeron is the **last pure Verdant** descendant.

**Ethereal**

- Attuned to **structure and clarity**: pattern recognition, disciplined purpose, symbolic logic.
- Expresses as calm under pressure, ability to see frameworks and long-arcs others miss.
- Lyra is the **last pure Ethereal** descendant.

**Union Theme**

- True Harmony is **relationship, not power**:
    - Verdant without Ethereal can drown in feeling without direction.
    - Ethereal without Verdant can become clarity weaponized into cruelty.
    - Aeron and Lyra’s connection is the world’s last credible attempt at reunion of these aspects.

**Implementation**

- All bloodline “magic” is **somatic, fallible, and never a clean mind-link.** (See resonance micro-events in Additions Pack for specific beats.)

### 2.7 Solveil Vertical Tiers & Naming

**Codex ID:** `solveil_vertical_tiers`

**Type:** City structure / spatial lore

**Core Canon**

Solveil is a vertically stratified city-state:

- **Aeries** – upper sanctum, council towers, clean air, curated quiet.
- **Spans** – the massive structural decks and transit arteries connecting the city’s heights to its depths.
- **Unders** – buried sectors, collapsed infrastructure, and reclaimed ruins where Ghostline thrives.

**Official Echelon Nomenclature vs Common Speech**

To keep dialogue and UI consistent:

- **Zone 0** (official) ↔ **the Unders** (common)
    - Treated by Infrastructure as hazardous waste zone; Order Division performs sporadic sweeps but avoids deep sectors.
- **Lower-Spans** (official) ↔ **“The Drop”** (common)
    - Vertigo-inducing elevator shafts, skeletal bridges, maintenance corridors connecting Wards to Unders. Favored by smugglers and Ghostrunners.
- **Foundry Wards** (official) ↔ **“The Belt”** (common)
    - Industrial mid-tiers born from the **Realignment** after a catastrophic Core failure. Split into multiple foundry districts under Infrastructure and Order oversight.
    - Heavy particulate air, stacked dorm blocks, 12-hour shifts, and propaganda that recasts past disaster as heroic sacrifice.
- **The Concourse** (official) ↔ **Upper Spans** (common)
    - Transit hubs, commercial promenades, and controlled access routes between Aeries towers and the rest of the city.

**Usage Rules**

- **In-world dialogue**: characters use common nicknames (“the Belt,” “the Drop,” “the Unders,” “Upper Spans”) unless they are speaking in official capacity (Council briefings, Echelon documentation).
- **Lore / UI**: can show both (“Foundry Wards / the Belt”) on first mention; then default to narrative flavor.

### 2.8 Glass, Ghost Hop, Carrier Hiss

**Codex IDs:**

- `glass_motif`
- `ghost_hop`
- `carrier_hiss`

**Glass (Aeron’s Moniker / Motif)**

- Whispered nickname for Aeron’s dissociative persona: apparently transparent, empty, and harmless—until pressure makes him sharp.
- Motif usage:
    - Inner shame as a distant metallic “ring”.
    - Externalized as faint environmental pings in key trauma or obedience scenes.

**Ghost Hop**

- A single, tightly packed data burst sent across a short Ghostline path.
- Designed to look like a one-off calibration spike to Echelon sensors, then vanish.

**Carrier Hiss**

- The natural wind-shear bandwidth around structures like Obsidian Bridge, used as acoustic/electromagnetic camouflage.
- Ghostline packets ride “under” this noise so they’re indistinguishable from environmental hum.

---

### 2.9 Courier Network & Rebellion Symbiosis

**Codex ID:** `courier_network`

**Type:** Human logistics under occupation

**Overview**

- A web of human couriers moving physical data (drives, paper, encoded objects) between tiers.
- Operates in **symbiosis** with Ghostline:
    - Ghostline carries transient, volatile intel.
    - Couriers carry **proof**—copies, manifests, signed fragments that survive outages or jamming.

**Relationship With Key Characters**

- Zira’s collaboration with Selene allows rebellion cells to synchronize:
    - Zira translates signal into actionable intel.
    - Selene folds it into strategy and assigns couriers to carry physical evidence.
- The pair are remembered underground as:
    - “They speak in code, we speak in courage. Together, that’s language.”

## 3) Timeline Anchors (Lore-Specific)

These anchors exist to keep lore-sensitive beats consistent across docs and scenes.

### 3.1 Bridge → Chip → Purge

- **Act I**
    - Aeron meets Zira and receives the **Beacon Chip** at Obsidian Bridge.
    - Aeron and Lyra **witness** the Purge of Sectors 8–10 with no prior knowledge. This is a fixed, non-branching event.
- **Act II**
    - Ghostline sync activates the chip: Aeron is now authenticated, and partial proof of Marcus’s Purge order surfaces.
    - OB vs EMP determine **order and framing**:
        - EMP: trust with Zira and Ghostline builds earlier.
        - OB: proof arrives later, under heavier guilt and denial.

---

### 3.2 Realignment & Foundry Wards

- **Pre-game**: a major Core regulator failure in the Belt causes a deadly industrial disaster.
- Echelon’s response:
    - Splits the region into tightly controlled **Foundry Districts** (the Wards).
    - Rewrites the event as heroic sacrifice in propaganda, institutionalizing inherited guilt among workers.
- The Wards become:
    - A laboratory for new surveillance and conditioning protocols.
    - A persistent scar that shapes Tessa’s background and early uprisings.
