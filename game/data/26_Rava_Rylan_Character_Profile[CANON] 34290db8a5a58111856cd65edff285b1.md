# 26_Rava_Rylan_Character_Profile[CANON]

**Born:** Approximately 356 AC, Foundry Wards (no formal birth record — the Wards were not registering Unders-side births in that year)

**Died:** 383 AC, Sector 10, during Echelon Operation 391 ("The Sweep"), age 27

**Relatives:** Kael Rylan (husband, dec. 381 AC Operation Stillwater), Soren Rylan (daughter, age 4 at time of death)

## 1. Foundry Wards origins

Rava was born Rava Osren in a Foundry Wards blockhouse and lost both parents before she was ten. She was raised by a courier collective that ran a laundry front on Tier 4 — an Unders-side operation older than Phoenix by a decade, providing safehouses and moving goods under the cover of a legitimate business. She grew up inside the ethic of the place: *keep the doors clean, keep the people moving, keep the questions small.* She never formally joined Phoenix. She worked the laundry.

## 2. Meeting Kael

Phoenix couriers used Rava's collective as a waypoint. Kael was one of them in 377–378 AC, moving intelligence from the Wards up to Sector-level drops. He was 22, she was 21. He noticed her the first visit. She noticed him the third visit. On the fifth visit he stayed an extra hour and asked her name. On the sixth visit she asked his.

He told her who he was on the tenth visit. The whole thing — the rooftop, the lie, Selene, the nine years he had been dead. She did not flinch. She had been working a laundry for seven years by then; she had a high tolerance for surprising information.

They married quietly in the Phoenix internal registry in 378 AC. Rava did not change her name in any public-facing document, because there were no public-facing documents for her to change. In Phoenix's private registry she was listed as **Rava Rylan**. She took the name because Kael had been forbidden his and she wanted him to have one of them back.

## 3. The wife Selene never knew

Rava met Selene Ward seven times in three years. Selene called her Rava. Rava called her Commander Ward. They did not dislike each other. They did not become close. Rava understood the cost of operational rank; Selene understood the cost of a wife who stayed out of operations. Each of them considered the other the keeper of Kael's other life.

Rava did not attend operational briefings. She did not handle intelligence. She kept a home in a quiet Phoenix safehouse and she made sure Kael came back to it. She was not a soldier. She was the reason Kael had something to come back to. Selene, in retrospect, called this a job. In the moment, neither woman called it anything.

## 4. Soren's birth and the contact with Liora

When Soren was born in 379 AC, Rava was the one who told Kael to reach out to his mother. She had never met Liora. She knew about her only through Kael's quiet references. But she knew, the way mothers know, that a grandchild was a thing that could travel across years of silence. She told him: *"Your mother should know she has a granddaughter. Even if she cannot come to us. Even if she never answers. Write to her. Tell her the girl's name."*

Kael wrote. Liora answered through the Outlands network, quietly, in a single line. Rava kept the answer. Rava was the one who filed Kael's reach-out as a contingency — *"if anything happens to Kael, Liora is the fallback."* She did not tell Selene. She did not tell Phoenix. She did what a Foundry Wards courier-collective girl had been trained to do: *keep the door clean, keep the people moving, keep the questions small.*

## 5. The survival test

Rava built her own teaching from Kael's story about his little brother. She had never met Aeron. She had only Kael's description — his face, his walk, the way he carried his shoulders when Marcus was watching. But she understood the shape of it: *a boy who survived the Aeries by being quiet, who was fine when nobody was looking at him, who was going to be fine as long as Marcus did not need him to be something he was not.* She turned the story into a survival test for Unders children: *some soldiers still got hearts, here is how you check.* She began teaching it to Soren at age three, the way she had been taught the laundry's door-and-window protocols at the same age.

She did not know, when she taught it, that the test was going to be applied to the person it had been written about.

## 6. Stillwater and the flight

Kael died at Stillwater in 381 AC. Rava received word from Selene's courier that night. She did not weep in front of Soren. She spent the night folding Kael's clothes. At dawn she packed two bags and walked her daughter out of Phoenix territory without telling Selene she was going.

She did not trust Phoenix to protect them. Phoenix had just lost the person it was built around. She did not trust the courier networks she had been working in because the couriers had lost Kael to an intelligence error and she did not know which error. She walked her two-year-old daughter into the Unders with one bag of clothes and one bag of laundry tools and settled in Sector 10 because Sector 10 was far enough from Phoenix that the sweep patterns did not map.

## 7. Sector 10

For two years — 381 to 383 AC — Rava and Soren lived in a tenement on the third floor of a Sector 10 block. Rava took work in a local laundry, the same work she had done in the Wards. She made no friends she could not afford to lose. She kept Soren quiet. She taught her to run her father's survival test. She kept one line of contact to a single Outlands-network courier, who passed her one message from Liora in 382 AC offering sanctuary in the Outlands. Rava refused the sanctuary. She wanted no more rebellion, no more Rylan anywhere. She wanted Soren raised out of the story. The courier did not press. The courier kept watching Sector 10 on standing order.

## 8. The Lower Spans recognition

Early 383 AC. A few days before Operation 391. Rava was walking Soren back from a water-collection point in the Lower Spans when she saw an Echelon reconnaissance officer moving alone between the pipes. She recognized him by the bones.

She had lived with Kael's face for five years. She knew the set of the Rylan jaw, the shape of the shoulders, the particular walking rhythm that the men in that family all shared whether they wanted to or not. The officer in the Lower Spans had all of it. She did not need a photograph. She had a wife's certainty, which is more reliable than any intelligence file and cannot be written down.

She was paralyzed. Her brother-in-law was leading reconnaissance in her sector. She had exactly one day's warning that a sweep was coming. She had three options: approach him and reveal herself, warn the block and trigger a panic that would out her, or hide. Rava was Foundry Wards. Her instinct, trained from childhood, was to hide. She did not trust any Rylan in Echelon service — she watched her husband escape that world at the cost of his own public death, and she would not walk her daughter into the same uniform. She pulled Soren back from the pipes before the child could say more than a few words, walked her home, and decided to wait out the reconnaissance and see if Aeron's team moved on.

That night, in the tenement, folding laundry because the fear needed somewhere to go, Rava told her four-year-old daughter: *"The soldier you talked to in the pipes — I think that was your uncle. Your father's brother. I think his name is Aeron."* That was the first time Soren heard the word *uncle* applied to a specific person. It was also the first time she heard the name *Aeron.* She did not fully understand the weight of either word. She was four. She stored them anyway, the way children store things they know are important.

## 9. Operation 391

The next morning the sweep came. Rava hid Soren in a basket of folded laundry in the back room of the tenement — the same kind of laundry basket Rava had grown up folding in the Foundry Wards — and told her to be quiet. She said: *"You'll be fine if you're quiet. Your uncle was fine when he was quiet."* This was the second time in her life Rava used the word *uncle* aloud in front of Soren, and Rava was now fully aware of which uncle she meant.

Rava was killed in the sweep. The squad cleared the room. The squad left. Within twenty minutes, the Outlands courier on standing watch entered the tenement and found Soren in the basket. Rava's body was on the floor. The courier did not have time to move the body. The courier moved the child.

## 10. What Rava left her daughter

- The word *uncle* attached to a specific face
- The test for soldiers who still had hearts
- The basket
- The instruction to be quiet
- A survival model based on her husband's love for his brother, unknowingly returned to the brother by the brother's niece
