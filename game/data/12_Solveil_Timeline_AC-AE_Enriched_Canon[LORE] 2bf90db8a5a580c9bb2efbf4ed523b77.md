## The Solveil Timeline (AC→AE) — Enriched Canon v2

**AC = After Consolidation**

**AE = After Echelon**

This timeline is a **high-level canon spine**, not a scene list. Use it to keep ages, eras, and big events consistent across all bibles and scripts. Moment-to-moment beats still live in act outlines and character docs.

---

### 0) Preludes (Old-World Context — Internal Only)

These dates are **pre-Solveil history**. They do not appear verbatim in-game; they’re for writer consistency.

- **2040–2080 (CE) — The Age of Collapse**
    
    Climate spiral, privatized protectorates, resource wars, mass migration. National borders become porous; corporate security replaces states.
    
- **2081–2095 (CE) — Corporate Alliance**
    
    Megacorps fuse emergency infrastructure to keep trade alive.
    
    The coalition that will become **Echelon** coalesces around “stability as product.”
    

---

### 1) Founding & Lockdown (1–100 AC)

**Solveil** is built on the ruins of old Chicago as the flagship “solution city.”

- **1 AC — The Consolidation Charter**
    
    Echelon becomes the governing entity. **Project Solveil** breaks ground atop drowned/ruined Chicago.
    
    - Integration Contract with the **Cathedral of Saint Solenne** (future Harmony Bureau):
        - Cathedral retains custodianship of **Harmony Cores**.
        - The state gains **exclusive operational use**.
    - The **Council of Archons** is constituted (Archon Prime + Majors/Minors).
    - *Official:* “Order begins.”
        
        *True:* Legitimacy is borrowed from Cathedral aura.
        
- **7–30 AC — The Building Years**
    
    First vertical districts rise; perimeter foundations and filtration towers come online. Solveil begins as a corporate ark, not yet the sealed fortress it becomes.
    
- **31 AC — The Lockdown Charter**
    
    Borders officially seal: “Nothing uncontrolled enters or leaves.”
    
    Micro-registration of citizens begins. Early language that will become the **Non-Population Doctrine** (legal erasure of the Unders) is seeded here.
    
- **50–70 AC — Harmony Cores Publicized**
    
    Harmony Cores are formalized as “discoveries.”
    
    Containment and calibration are ritualized as science; the Cathedral’s mystique is reframed as “applied resonance engineering.”
    
- **62 AC — Harmonic Constitution Ratified**
    
    Defines **Recognized Citizen** as one whose “life metrics contribute to civic stability.”
    
    Later, Section 4.3.2 becomes the legal basis for omitting the Unders from all records.
    
- **95 AC — The Branding Program**
    
    Biometric / resonance identification becomes mandatory.
    
    The **Harmony Bureau** oversees all rites, locking spiritual authority and civic control into a single ritual pipeline.
    

---

### 2) Expansion, Industrialization & Uprisings (101–200 AC)

Solveil grows teeth.

- **120–160 AC — Industrial Expansion**
    
    The **Foundry Wards** are established; Solveil reaches energy independence through continuous industrial output.
    
- **171 AC — The Middle-Tier Uprisings**
    
    Housing and food riots erupt in the middle tiers. Roughly **40,000** die in Order “peace operations.”
    
    - The **Technology, Research & Development Division (TR&D)** is formally authorized as a core arm of Echelon.
    - Inside TR&D, the **Cognitive Bureau** (behavior research and control) is spun up to detect pre-defection behavior.
    - **RRT (Resonance Response Testing)** is prototyped in industrial cohorts as a “stability metric.”
- **188–190 AC — Citywide Behavioral Regimen**
    
    RRT begins rolling beyond Foundry pilots; “variance” becomes a civic risk metric across the city. Citizens are no longer just measured by output but by internal alignment.
    

---

### 3) Second Consolidation & Hardening (201–300 AC)

The system stops apologizing and starts optimizing.

- **190–210 AC — The Second Consolidation (“Year of Noise”)**
    
    Rebuild + narrative rewrite after unrest.
    
    The Cathedral’s **Stillness doctrine** is secularized into civic virtue: quiet, compliance, and predictability become moralized.
    
- **205–213 AC — Sediment Beat: The Audit Schism (Bureau vs Council)**
    
    The Harmony Bureau uses archival access and Branding oversight as leverage against the Council of Archons.
    
    - Ends with **tighter Bureau sign-off** on Core relocations and rites.
    - *Consequence:* Explains the Bureau’s enduring veto power over certain state actions.
- **223 AC — Perimeter Militarization**
    
    The **External Aegis Line** is hardened; the **Military Division** gains autonomy over Outlands operations. Patrols shift from defensive to pre-emptive containment.
    
- **240–260 AC — Technological Golden Age**
    
    Under TR&D, Echelon deploys:
    
    - Neural-interface drones
    - Resonance-based weapons
    - Near-total urban automation
        
        Solveil becomes a highly automated machine with humans as monitored components.
        
- **247 AC — Sediment Beat: First Drone Mutiny (software fault)**
    
    Unintended swarm behavior causes citywide panic.
    
    - TR&D purges codebases and enforces strict firmware standardization.
    - *Consequence:* Sets precedent for intrusive system-wide audits and justifies further RRT creep.
- **272 AC — Courier Seeds**
    
    First hand-to-hand courier cells form in the Wards.
    
    Slogan: **“Voice over order.”** This is the proto-network that Selene will eventually inherit.
    
- **300–320 AC — Ghostline Emergence**
    
    The Unders coalesce into a digital underworld.
    
    Early relay meshes, improvised from industrial scrap and forgotten maintenance channels, precede Zira’s later Ghostline.

### 4) Preludes to the Cast (332–352 AC)

The city settles into its “normal” dystopia. Subtle faults accumulate.

- **332–336 AC — Sediment Beat: Substrate Shortage (Foundry)**
    
    Micro-fab materials run thin; infrastructure rationing and Order curfews hit the Wards hard.
    
    - Black-market routes bloom.
    - *Consequence:* Social routing habits (hidden paths, trusted smugglers) later exploited by Couriers.
- **347–350 AC — Sediment Beat: Bandwidth Famine**
    
    Citywide data throttling forces maintenance crews and the Unders to improvise.
    
    - Data gray markets explode.
    - **Ghostline-style** channels normalize as “off-grid” comms under the mid-tiers.
- **351–352 AC — Sediment Beat: Archon Succession Scare**
    
    A contested appointment triggers “temporary” emergency powers for the Order Division.
    
    - Those powers never fully sunset.
    - *Consequence:* Sets the texture of Aeron’s formative years — Order in domestic ascendancy, fear as routine.

### 5) The Generation of the LIs (353–361 AC)

Ages are given as **@ 383 AC (story start) / 391 AC (Fall).**

- **353 AC — Selene Ward born (30 / 38)**
    
    Born in a bunker cell; raised by the cause. The rebellion is her childhood and her religion.
    
- **355 AC — Noelle Korr born (28 / 36)**
    
    Upper-tier prodigy earmarked early for the Cognitive Bureau under TR&D.
    
- **358 AC — Zira born (25 / 33)**
    
    Sector 12-B, Unders. Scrapper-tech family that treats broken machines like puzzles, not trash.
    
- **360 AC — Aeron Rylan, Lyra Althea, Tessa Kael born (23 / 31)**
    - Aeron: Aeries-adjacent military household, raised in the shadow of Marcus.
    - Lyra: Cathedral-aligned Ethereal lineage.
    - Tessa: Foundry child, born into the Belt’s industrial grind.

### 6) Character-Defining Incidents (372–382 AC)

These are the **pre-game fractures** that shape who we meet in Act I.

- **372 AC — Lyra’s Rite of Stillness (Age 12)**
    
    Lyra is “touched” by Core synchrony during her rite.
    
    - Officially framed as a holy affirmation.
    - Functionally, it brands her as an ideal **Vessel** for the Harmony Bureau’s long-term plans.
- **376 AC — Wing Seven Collapse (Lyra 16)**
    
    Lyra’s mentor **Elisse** dies shielding her during a containment failure.
    
    - Official report: “impurity incident.”
    - True cause: infrastructure failure + doctrinal cover-up.
    - Trauma: Lyra internalizes that people die when she fails to be perfect.
- **376–377 AC — Lyra Transferred to Marcus Rylan**
    
    Lyra is reassigned from Cathedral tutelage to Marcus’s command orbit.
    
    - Faith language is repurposed as command discipline.
    - Her belief in harmony is rewritten as belief in order.
- **377–379 AC — Zira’s Relay Years**
    
    Zira and her older brother **Renn** build community comms in the Unders — the seed of Ghostline.
    
- **378 AC — Aeron Enters Service (Age 18)**
    
    Joins the **Military Division**.
    
    - Service window: **378–385 AC**.
    - Precision record begins: nearly flawless operations, minimal collateral — the making of “Glass.”
- **378–379 AC — The Breach (Zira 20–21)**
    
    Echelon raid destroys Zira’s encampment.
    
    - Zira’s left eardrum ruptured; Renn executed on feed by General Marcus Rylan.
    - Zira salvages Renn’s cracked wafer and begins rebuilding **Ghostline-Theta** from it.
- **379–381 AC — Selene’s Network Consolidation (Age 26–28)**
    
    Selene unifies scattered courier cells into a coherent **Courier Network**.
    
    - Doctrine: “Command Temperature” — making hard calls without flinching.
- **379–382 AC — Noelle’s Ascent and Fall (Age 24–27)**
    
    Noelle becomes cognitive architect of **NDI** (Neural Disposition Index) under TR&D.
    
    - Discovers selective archival edits — the “Deleted Ones.”
    - Defects with corrupted data crystal.
    - Official story: “voluntary euthanasia.”
    - Truth: she ran.

### 7) The Story Window (383–391 AC)

This is the **active VN window** — everything we write must thread into this backbone.

### 383 AC — Story Start

- Ages: Aeron / Lyra / Tessa 23; Zira 25; Noelle 28; Selene 30.
- **Inspection Day**: Aeron’s prior behavior sets his RRT flag.
    - OB lean → clean report.
    - EMP lean → variance triggered.

### Late 383 AC — Sector 8–10 Purge (Act I Climax)

Mass “containment” escalates to execution across Sectors 8–10.

- Aeron and Lyra **do not know in advance**; witnessing the purge from above is their disillusion threshold.
- **Nyra Vale**, an Order lieutenant, experiences doctrine break as command logic contradicts itself. This begins the spiral that ends in her defection (age deliberately left flexible here).
- **Tessa** tends civilians under live fire — “the hand that stayed,” anchoring her as moral center amid state violence.

### 383 AC — Obsidian Bridge Meet

- First true **Aeron × Zira** contact at Obsidian Bridge in Sector 10.
- Ghostline trust test; Zira reads Aeron as the kind of man Renn *might* have become.

### 384–385 AC — Final Missions & Break

- Aeron completes his ledger with **391 total missions** (multi-count ops).
- **385 AC — Defection (Aeron 25)**
    - Leaves the mirror of Marcus.
    - Carries Zira’s **ghost-beacon chip** — dormant but symbolic.

### 385–391 AC — The Six-Year Rebellion

Phased for writer reference:

- **385–386 AC — The Concord Phase**
    
    Aeron enters Selene’s lattice; **Couriers + Ghostline** unify (Greenline / Amberline / Redline cadence).
    
    Lyra’s faith deconstructs under Bureau falsifications; she chooses **connection over creed**.
    
- **386–388 AC — Perimeter Wars (Quiet)**
    
    Sabotage of drone stacks, Foundry liberations, and data exfiltration from **TR&D nodes**.
    
    Noelle proves Echelon edits its own failures — **truth as weapon.**
    
- **388–389 AC — Order Fractures**
    
    RRT backfires under mass noise; variance flags flood systems.
    
    Nyra’s devotion either **hardens (OB)** or **softens (EMP)**, reframing her Veilbreaker oath around Aeron instead of Echelon.
    
- **390 AC — The Aegis Stutters**
    
    Infrastructure load-shedding events cascade; the Harmony Bureau loses veto leverage in the panic.
    
    Public myth of Outlands uninhabitability begins cracking (sensor leaks, Ghostline captures).
    
- **391 AC — Fall of Echelon (Aeron 31)**
    
    The year number mirrors **391 missions** in Aeron’s ledger.
    
    City Core control severs; Council dispersed; Military/Order parity collapses.
    
    **AE Era begins.**

### 9) Population & Space (Reference)

Quick “feel” numbers for scale and pacing.

- **Aeries:** ~12% of counted population.
    
    Natural sun filtered through polarized solar arrays; curated light, curated people.
    
- **Middle Tiers:** ~55%
    
    “Normal life” under doctrine — work, commute, filtered sky.
    
- **Foundry Wards:** ~33%
    
    Industrial backbone; controlled-collapse zones where accidents are policy tools.
    
- **Unders (Zone 0):**
    
    Unrecorded and undefined by law and census. If records exist, they’re in **TR&D black archives** or not at all.
