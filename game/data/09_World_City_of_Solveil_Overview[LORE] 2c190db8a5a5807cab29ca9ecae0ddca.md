## 10_World_City_Of_Solveil_Overview[LORE]

### 0. Purpose & Scope

This document is the **one-stop overview of the city of Solveil**:

- What Solveil is as a **place** (theme, tone, function in the story).
- The **vertical tier structure** (Aeries, Mid, Unders) and how life differs between them.
- Key **infrastructure systems** (power, transit, surveillance, communication).
- Important **districts and landmarks** that recur across routes.
- How Solveil relates to **Echelon**, the **Phoenix network**, and the wider world.

Other docs (Echelon structure, Phoenix overview, timeline, etc.) should **reference this** instead of re-explaining basic city facts.

---

## 1. Solveil at a Glance

- **Type:** Walled, hyper-stratified city-state.
- **Function:** Industrial, logistical, and ideological hub for Echelon’s power.
- **Population:** Millions, heavily concentrated in the mid and lower tiers.
- **Tone:**
    - *Vertical cruelty* — comfort and safety literally increase with altitude.
    - *Managed decay* — the Unders are allowed to rot so they can be controlled.
    - *Manufactured inevitability* — propaganda paints Solveil as the only surviving “order” worth having.

Solveil is not just a backdrop; it is **the machine** that grinds characters down:

- For **Aeron**, the city is a cage he’s been trained to maintain.
- For **Lyra**, it’s a system she’s sworn to keep from collapsing—even if the cost is horrific.
- For **Zira**, it’s the crime scene of her entire life.
- For **Tessa**, it’s a bleeding patient she keeps trying to stabilize.
- For **Selene and Noelle**, it’s a problem to be solved with strategy or truth.

## 2. Vertical Structure: Aeries, Mid, Unders

### 2.1 The Aeries – Upper Tier

**Summary:** Elevated spires, elite enclaves, and Echelon’s command centers.

- **Physical Environment**
    - High-altitude towers, skybridges, and aerie platforms suspended above smog and haze.
    - Clean, filtered air; muted city noise; controlled lighting and climate.
    - Heavy use of glass, polished alloys, and controlled greenery (curated gardens, not wild growth).
- **Population & Power**
    - Echelon leadership, high-ranking officers, TR&D executives, favored families, and propaganda faces.
    - Access to the best medical care, cognitive treatments, and security protections.
    - Strict internal surveillance — loyalty is rewarded, but everyone is watched.
- **Everyday Life**
    - Silent drones along skybridges; biometric checkpoints at building entries.
    - Social events like the **gala** Aeron attends — political theatre disguised as celebration.
    - Most Aeries citizens only know the Unders through controlled feeds and cautionary stories.
- **Narrative Use**
    - Stage for **Echelon rituals** (promotions, purges framed as “justice,” public address broadcasts).
    - Anchor for Aeron’s internal conflict: he moves through polished halls built on unseen bodies.
    - Contrast backdrop for **his mother’s fate** in the Empathy path (executed from the world he once called “home”).

---

### 2.2 The Mid – Industrial & Residential Tiers

**Summary:** The “functional” city — factories, worker housing, transit hubs, and propaganda.

- **Physical Environment**
    - Dense layers of concrete, steel, and stacked housing blocks.
    - Industrial corridors threaded with cargo trams and conveyors.
    - Smog, noise, and constant vibration from machinery above and below.
- **Population & Control**
    - Majority of Solveil’s registered workers live here: operators, technicians, low-level officers, service staff.
    - Constant scheduling and quotas; people are tracked through work shifts and rations.
    - Security presence is heavy but “professional” — patrols, checkpoints, ID scans.
- **Everyday Life**
    - Loudspeakers broadcasting Echelon’s messaging: productivity, gratitude, fear of the Unders.
    - State-issued entertainment, ration queues, controlled markets.
    - Housing blocks with thin walls, flickering lights, and occasional blackouts quickly “resolved” to maintain calm.
- **Narrative Use**
    - Transitional space where Aeron can see both **the lie** (posters, staged safety briefings) and **the cost** (injury, burnout, silent resentment).
    - Neutral ground where **Phoenix sympathizers** quietly move information and supplies.
    - Area where characters like **Tessa** can plausibly work in clinics/repair wards serving both workers and Echelon’s casualties.

---

### 2.3 The Unders – Collapse, Shadow, and Resistance

**Summary:** Crushed infrastructure, forgotten sectors, and the breeding ground of rebellion.

- **Physical Environment**
    - Sunlight rarely reaches the ground; illumination comes from flickering signs, jury-rigged lamps, and fires.
    - Flooded tunnels, broken transit lines, and half-collapsed blocks.
    - Improvised bridges (like the **Obsidian Bridge**) spanning chasms opened by structural failures or deliberate demolitions.
- **Population & Survival**
    - Unregistered residents, displaced workers, smuggling networks, and informal communities.
    - Gangs, resistance cells, and independent operators like Zira’s old encampment.
    - People live in salvaged rooms, old maintenance corridors, abandoned stations.
- **Everyday Life**
    - Barter economies; makeshift clinics (e.g., Tessa’s worksites), underground eateries and markets.
    - Constant threat of Echelon raids, sweeps, and “purges” framed as “infrastructure safety operations.”
    - Local codes of honor and reputation that matter more than official law.
- **Narrative Use**
    - Primary setting for **Zira’s introduction**, Unders raids, and the **Sector 8–10 purge** Aeron and Lyra witness.
    - Physical expression of everything Aeron’s been trained to ignore — and what the Empathy path forces him to face.
    - The only place where the **Phoenix network** can truly exist as a community rather than a rumor.

## 3. Infrastructure & Systems

### 3.1 Power & Utilities

- Solveil relies on centralized, highly controlled power systems feeding each tier.
- Mid-tier industrial plants generate much of the energy; distribution downward to the Unders is inconsistent and often weaponized:
    - Blackouts in the Unders can be **punishment**, **control**, or **cover** for operations.
- Basic water and sanitation services in the Mid/Aeries are stable; in the Unders they are patchwork, community-maintained, or failing.

**Narrative hooks:**

- Sabotaging or protecting a power relay can change **world state tags** (`heat_*`, `unders_*`) via mission outcomes.
- Clinics & safehouses in the Unders often run on stolen or jerry-rigged power — easy targets for Echelon.

---

### 3.2 Transit & Access

- **Vertical transit:** Elevators, freight lifts, and restricted shaft-lines connect Mid to Aeries; most are heavily controlled by Echelon.
- **Horizontal transit:** Cargo trams, service tunnels, skybridges, and maintenance corridors.
- Many former official routes in the Unders are collapsed or blocked; locals create new paths (ladders, catwalks, rope bridges).

**Narrative hooks:**

- Control of a **shaft or access node** can mean control of information and movement between tiers.
- Zira’s skills and ghostline network are often used to **spoof access**, open locked passages, or reroute drones.

---

### 3.3 Surveillance & Communications

- Echelon runs a layered surveillance network:
    - Drones patrolling the Mid and Unders.
    - Cameras and environmental sensors in public corridors.
    - Data fusion handled by TR&D’s internal systems and the Cognitive Division’s behavioral models.
- In the Aeries, surveillance doubles as social pressure — people *perform loyalty*.
- The Unders are under-wired but over-policed:
    - Coverage is patchy, but when something matters, Echelon **floods** the area with drones and temporary stations.
    - Gaps in coverage allow networks like Zira’s ghostlines to exist.

**Narrative hooks:**

- `world_state()` tags like `heat_elevated`, `heat_high`, `heat_critical` map directly to how intense this surveillance feels in scenes.
- Missions that spike `team_suspicion` and `echelon_alert` translate into more oppressive city description and fewer safe routes.

But these names should be consistent:

### 4.1 Key Sectors

- **Sector 7**
    - Mid-to-lower industrial zone tied to Lyra’s past operations and early commendations.
    - Associated with **her first major “success” and first real trauma** (lives lost for the sake of stability).
    - Serves as a ghost in her story — referenced in Act I and revisited emotionally in Act II/III.
- **Sector 8–10 Corridor**
    - Unders expanse with overlapping communities, smuggling routes, and unstable infrastructure.
    - Targeted for the **purge** Aeron and Lyra witness from above.
    - Houses safehouses, community centers, and informal networks that Phoenix heavily relies on.
- **Inner Industrial Sectors (Mid)**
    - Heavy manufacturing, logistics nodes, refinery stacks.
    - Where the day-to-day “engine of Solveil” lives and dies.
    - Often used as staging grounds for both Echelon operations and Phoenix sabotage.

---

### 4.2 Signature Landmarks

- **Obsidian Bridge**
    - Abandoned, precarious bridge in the Unders chosen as Zira’s meeting point with Aeron.
    - Overlooks chasms of collapsed city; visually embodies “standing over the void” between obedience and empathy.
    - Becomes a symbolic location for **trust, betrayal, and the first real cracks** in Aeron’s obedience.
- **Gala Hall / Aeries Memorial Spaces**
    - Highly controlled spaces where Echelon performs its own myths.
    - The **gala** and later memorial scenes (e.g., Selene’s “funeral” in OB path) show how the regime **rewrites truth in real time**.
- **Phoenix Clinics & Safehouses (various)**
    - Hidden in the mid/low tiers and Unders: back rooms of shops, basements of collapsed buildings, tunnels.
    - Tessa’s work, Selene’s planning, and Zira’s tech rigs often converge here.
    - Their stability and safety can degrade over the story depending on mission outcomes and purges.

## 5. Everyday Life & Social Hierarchy

### 5.1 Living Under Echelon

- **Aeries citizens** live in curated environments, insulated from mess and danger.
- **Mid workers** grind through schedules, queues, and metrics, targeted by both propaganda and punishment.
- **Unders residents** are officially “problems to be contained” rather than citizens to be served.

Common threads:

- **Fear of the Unders** is deliberately cultivated in the Mid and Aeries.
- **Fear of the Aeries** is a survival reflex in the Unders.
- Mid-tier citizens often rationalize the suffering below as “necessary” — a belief Aeron has to interrogate.

---

### 5.2 Culture, Rumors, & Resistance

- Stories about **Sector collapses, past purges, and missing people** circulate differently in each tier.
- The **Phoenix network** navigates these stories:
    - In the Aeries: whispered doubts in private rooms.
    - In the Mid: rumors on break shifts and in black markets.
    - In the Unders: direct, lived experience of raids and retaliation.

The way people **talk about Solveil** (and what they refuse to talk about) is itself a measure of control.

---

## 6. Solveil & the Wider World

Solveil positions itself as:

- The **last functioning center of order**, surrounded by decayed or fragmented regions.
- The gateway through which resources, tech, and information are controlled.

What lies beyond the walls is:

- Real but **deliberately obscured** in propaganda.
- A mix of ruined cities, scattered settlements, and other power structures that never fully materialize in early Acts—but can be referenced for:
    - Echelon war stories.
    - Phoenix long-term goals (not just freeing Solveil, but what comes after).

This doc intentionally leaves **external geopolitics** light; more detailed handling goes into a separate `Solveil_And_Wider_World[LORE]` doc if you choose to create it, to avoid bloat here.
