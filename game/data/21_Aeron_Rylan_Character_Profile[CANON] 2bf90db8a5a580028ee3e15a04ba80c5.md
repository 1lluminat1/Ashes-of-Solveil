## 1. Snapshot

**Name:** Aeron Rylan

**Age at story start:** 23 (born 360 AC; story starts 383 AC)

**Role:** Protagonist; last pure Verdant lineage heir; son of General Marcus Rylan.

**Core Concept:** A precision-trained officer of Echelon’s Military Division who was supposed to become his father’s perfect successor, but whose embedded empathy refuses to die.

**Persona:**

- **Glass** — dissociative shell; “transparent, empty, sharp when needed.”
- **Emerging self** — Verdant core, attuned to living systems and other people’s pain.

**Path Expression:**

- **OB:** Protective withholding, mission cadence, ritual control; guilt is delayed, compartmentalized, and leaks out as cracks later.
- **EMP:** Risk-forward compassion, earlier moral pushback, accepts exposure and consequences sooner.

**Motifs:**

- **Glass / Reflection / Windows**
- **Pulse / Heartbeat / Breath**
- **Verdant light / living systems**

**Path Hooks (from Paths Bible):**

- **OB:** Cleaner ops; trust slower; emotional debts accrue.
- **EMP:** Higher emotional “heat”; bonds strengthen earlier; intimacy and honesty unlock sooner.

## 2. Core Premise

> “Raised as a weapon, born as Verdant, trying to remember he’s allowed to be a person.”
> 

Aeron’s entire arc is a tug-of-war between:

- **Filial duty** to Marcus, Echelon, and the idea of “order through control.”
- **Lineage and instinct** as the last pure Verdant — someone naturally attuned to living systems, empathy, and the cost of harm.
- **The Glass persona**, a survival strategy forged by ritualized shame and child-soldier conditioning.

The VN’s core question is basically:

> If someone like Aeron is given power and love, does he become a better general… or something else entirely?
>

## 3. Biography & Timeline

### 3.1 Origins — Aeries Child

- **Birth:** 360 AC, in mid-level Aeries family quarters reserved for high-ranking officers’ families.
- **Parents:**
    - **Marcus Rylan** — rising star of the Military Division.
    - **Mother** — civilian-turned-Echelon spouse who *later escapes* as Marcus consolidates power (details in her own profile; for Aeron this is: “mother gone, never properly explained”).
- He grows up surrounded by **sterile perfection**: polished corridors, quiet security, framed battle commendations. The Aeries *look* like peace; underneath, they’re built on buried violence.

From the Solveil & Echelon deep dive:

> “Aeron was born here — in the Aeries mid-level quarters reserved for high-ranking officers’ families. His upbringing among sterile perfection explains both his detachment and his internal dissonance. The Aeries embody Echelon’s illusion of transcendence; his journey begins there because it’s the lie he has to unlearn.”
> 

### 3.2 Verdant Lineage & Early Conditioning

Canon: Aeron is the **last pure Verdant** line.

- **Verdant:** attuned to living systems; embodied empathy; senses “health” and “strain” in people, spaces, and systems as a kind of intuitive read.
- **Echelon’s Misread:** The regime treats Verdant/Ethereal as *weaponizable traits* instead of relational harmony. Marcus sees Aeron’s Verdant instinct as a dangerous “soft edge” that must be disciplined out of him.

Childhood beats (high level):

- Early on, Aeron is responsive to plants, animals, and people in distress; he tries to comfort, stabilize, help.
- Marcus rewards discipline and punishes “hesitation” or “weakness” — those moments where Aeron *fails* to carry out orders quickly because he’s checking on someone.

This creates the core wound: **Verdant instinct vs filial duty.**

### 3.3 Branding & the Birth of Glass

One of the key formative traumas: the **branding ritual**.

- Location: lower-tier facility used for indoctrination and punishment.
- Pretext: A “purification” rite to mark him as truly Echelon—loyal to the Council and to Marcus’s Military doctrine.
- Reality: Ritualized humiliation and pain; Aeron is restrained, branded with an Echelon sigil that scars across skin (canonical placement: shoulder/torso; exact visual in Art Bible).

Key emotional pieces:

- The ritual is framed as his *fault* — for emotional “imprecision,” childhood disobedience, or questioning.
- His **brother** is present in some capacity (either in earlier drills or the surrounding events), and the trauma memory later mixes **distorted hymn, smoke, and his brother’s face** (“Branding Echo”).
- In memory, the sound design carries a warped hymn and echoing footsteps; visuals repeatedly cut to his reflection in metal or glass.

**Glass** solidifies here as a persona:

- He learns to go inward, blank out, obey.
- “Glass” becomes a whispered moniker among some in the ranks: someone transparent, unreadable, sharp when needed, but “empty” behind the eyes.

### 3.4 Service Record — The Officer Marcus Wanted

Timeline anchors:

- **378–385 AC:** Enters Military service at 18; accrues a precision-perfect record for seven years.
- Logged as **391 total missions** by the time of the rebellion’s end — the number becomes symbolic later (391 missions → 391 AE, the year Echelon falls).
- During this period, he is a **model officer**:
    - Executes orders cleanly.
    - Rarely questions collateral.
    - Gains a reputation as “Marcus’s mirror” on the battlefield: same efficiency, less visible cruelty.

Internally, though:

- Glass keeps him functioning, but his Verdant empathy never fully dies.
- He notices the bodies, the fear, the small deviations in people’s breath and posture.
- These observations are shoved into silence rather than spoken; this is why when he *does* eventually defect, it feels sudden to others but inevitable to him.

### 3.5 Story Start — 383 AC

At VN start (383 AC):

- Aeron is 23.
- He’s already a trusted operative under Marcus’s command, with a clean file and clear upward trajectory.
- Outwardly: calm, precise, polite. The perfect Aeries-bred officer.
- Inwardly: micro-cracks — unease at “clean” operations, flickers of dissonance when he sees the Unders in person, sharp flashes of empathy he suppresses.

Act I key beats that define his headspace:

1. **Gala → Balcony:** He performs the role expected of him in public; micro-fractures appear in private balcony conversation, especially around duty vs self.
2. **The Message:** Marcus’s purge order hits his terminal; rain + terminal glow + reflection motif.
3. **Quiet Rooftop Reflection → Brother Memory:** Alone, his Glass persona slips; memory of last conversation with his brother surfaces.
4. **Branding Echo:** The rooftop memory links into the branding trauma — distorted hymn, brother’s face, the lag in his reflection.

### 3.6 The Purge & the Obsidian Bridge

**Sector 8–10 Purge (late 383 AC):**

- Canon: Aeron and Lyra **do not** know about the planned purge ahead of time.
- They go in expecting “containment”; they get massacre.
- This becomes the **disillusion threshold**: the moment their shared faith in Echelon fractures.

During/after the Purge:

- Aeron sees Tessa in Sector 10, staying with the dying under live fire. Her refusal to abandon people lodges in his mind as a counter-example to Echelon’s “acceptable loss” logic.
- His own hesitation and revulsion at the civilian deaths form the emotional seed for later defection.

**Obsidian Bridge — Midnight Meet (Act I):**

- Zira dictates the meeting location; Aeron arrives.
- In EMP, he lowers his weapon and engages verbally, earning a tiny trust increment. Zira marks him as “maybe salvageable.”
- In OB, he holds formation and keeps the mission voice; Zira logs him as “predictable compliance,” but the **contradictions** (small hesitations, micro-acts) keep her interested.

Importantly: **Zira’s chip** always ends up in Aeron’s possession, marking him as a latent ally in both routes.

### 3.7 Defection & the Six-Year Rebellion (High-Level)

- **385 AC (Aeron age 25):** Defection. Leaves Marcus, carrying Zira’s ghostline beacon.
- **385–391 AC:** The Six-Year Rebellion:
    - Aligns with Selene’s courier lattice and Zira’s Ghostline.
    - Becomes a central operator in the Concord Phase, where disparate cells unify.
    - Ways this plays later:
        - EMP: leans toward protecting people, bending ops to minimize harm.
        - OB: leans toward “clean” outcomes, often sacrificing civilians for tactical advantage.

The VN will cover the emotional, romantic, and ethical trajectory inside this period, from initial cracks to whichever version of “Aeron at 391” the player builds.

## 4. Psychological Profile

### 4.1 Wounds

From Trauma & Drama Bible:

- **Filial duty deformation:** He was raised to see his own worth as synonymous with usefulness to Marcus and Echelon.
- **Ritualized shame (branding):** Branded as punishment; taught that his pain is proof of loyalty.
- **Learned dissociation (Glass):** Emotional shutdown as survival; he compartmentalizes until his own reactions feel alien.

Additive wounds:

- **Mother’s absence:** She left/run from Echelon when he was very young. The story he was fed is likely “she was weak” or “she couldn’t handle the reality of order.” That abandonment fuses with his duty complex: “Do not be like her.”
- **Brother’s fate:** Whatever the exact cause (branding sequence, operation, punishment), Aeron associates his brother’s memory with Echelon ritual and failure. The rooftop memory + branding echo embed “I should have done something” deep in his psyche.

### 4.2 Core Fears

- Becoming **Marcus** — a man who can watch the world burn and call it stability.
- Becoming **nothing** — if he stops being useful, he fears he has no right to exist.
- Hurting the people he loves *because* he chose mercy or *because* he chose obedience — the double-bind at the heart of the paths.

### 4.3 Defenses

- **Glass persona:** Calm, precise, detached. Speaks in mission cadence, uses formal phrasing, avoids “I feel” language.
- **Perfectionism:** Obsessive about clean execution; failures are catastrophized.
- **Self-erasure:** In arguments, he defaults to “whatever you need me to be,” especially around authority figures (Marcus, Selene in early EMP, Nyra in OB).

### 4.4 Moral Compass

- At baseline, Aeron is not a sadist or a fanatic. He *wants* things to make sense and people to be safe.
- **OB:** He buys into the idea that only ruthless control will prevent worse chaos; he sacrifices smaller groups “for the city.” Guilt is buried, then leaks sideways into mental collapse.
- **EMP:** He risks destabilizing plans to save actual people; he believes that if the rebellion doesn’t protect the vulnerable, it doesn’t deserve to win.

## 5. Relationship Map (High-Level)

### 5.1 Marcus Rylan (Father / Antagonist)

- Marcus sees Aeron as both **legacy** and **experiment**: a chance to prove his Military doctrine is superior.
- Aeron internalizes “precision = love”; the better he performs, the less Marcus has to hurt him “for his own good.”
- Mirror motif: post-holo scenes often show Aeron’s reflection lagging or misaligned, indicating the gap between who Marcus thinks he is and who he’s becoming.

### 5.2 Mother (Name TBD; The One Who Left)

(These beats are new canon we outlined together; they’ll be fleshed more in her own profile.)

- She escaped Echelon shortly after Aeron was born to get out from under Marcus’s rising power.
- Aeron grows up with an engineered narrative about her — coward, traitor, or simply “gone.”
- **EMP Path:**
    - She eventually gets pulled back into Echelon’s crosshairs because of Aeron’s actions/associations.
    - Marcus is present for her public execution, stone-cold and observing; Aeron is forced to witness.
    - Execution is “300-style”: bound, taken to the edge of an Aeries structure, and pushed into the abyss — a hyper-theatrical message to the Unders.
    - Crucially, this is not random cruelty; it’s tied to a compassionate decision Aeron made earlier (e.g., helping a courier group), making it a devastating *cost of empathy* moment.
- **OB Path:**
    - She survives long enough to hear about what her son has become.
    - When they finally cross paths, she doesn’t cling or forgive. She spits at his feet, calls him his father’s son, maybe even slaps him, then *chooses* to walk away.
    - This is OB’s mirror to EMP’s execution: in one path he loses her body, in the other he loses her acceptance.

### 5.3 Lyra Althea (Ethereal Counterpart)

- Lyra is the last pure Ethereal; together they form the **Verdant/Ethereal union** that can rekindle Harmony.
- She sees both his discipline and his self-erasure, reading his distance as a form of slow suicide.
- **OB:** She experiences his obedience as abandonment; turning point is the “surrender of control” scene where she chooses messy love over sterile perfection.
- **EMP:** Early vulnerability and mutual confession; their path is about choosing connection over doctrine.

### 5.4 Zira (Ghostline)

- Zira reads Aeron as “the kind of man Renn could’ve become” — an echo of someone she lost.
- She tests him ruthlessly at Obsidian Bridge and afterward; trust builds from competence → banter → vulnerability.
- She is the one who forces him to confront the *practical* consequences of his choices in the Unders.

### 5.5 Tessa (The Hand That Stayed)

- Tessa is the first person he sees **refusing** to abandon the dying in the Purge.
- She becomes his medbay anchor: treats his wounds, calls out his avoidant guilt, teaches him grounding techniques.
- In OB, her warmth becomes friction against his growing rigidity; in EMP, her care pulls him out of Glass and into embodied feeling.

### 5.6 Noelle (Truth-Seeker)

- Noelle analyzes him as an anomaly: a soldier whose choices don’t match his conditioning.
- In EMP, his honesty and paradoxes fascinate her, pulling her toward real intimacy.
- In OB, she logs him as “predictable compliance” until his contradictions finally force her to adjust her models — or break from him.

### 5.7 Selene (The Rebellion Made Flesh)

- Selene recognizes his tactical brilliance and sees the risk of letting him command without heart.
- EMP: she slowly allows him into shared command, learning to balance her doctrine with his empathy.
- OB: she becomes the moral spine he ultimately breaks in killing her; that stain never leaves him.

### 5.8 Nyra (OB-Only LI / Anti-Mirror of Lyra)

- Nyra validates his most dangerous impulses: “You were made for this. You’re finally not lying to yourself.”
- She represents the path where Aeron stops fighting his training and instead crowns it.
- Their connection is real but destructive: she loves the version of him that embraces the machine.

## 6. Path Expressions — OB vs EMP

### 6.1 OB (Obedience) Path

- **Voice:** Inner monologue is clipped, procedural, often in second-person imperative (“Move. Aim. Don’t think.”).
- **Behavior:**
    - Follows Marcus/Selene/Nyra orders more strictly.
    - Accepts civilian casualties more often “for the mission.”
    - Avoids emotional conversations until they explode.
- **Arc:**
    - Early: “I can carry this so they don’t have to.”
    - Mid: Increasing numbness; begins to need harsher stimuli (violence, Nyra’s validation) to feel anything.
    - Late: Guilt crashes through; Glass cracks under the weight of what he’s done (Selene’s murder, mother’s rejection, etc.).

### 6.2 EMP (Empathy) Path

- **Voice:** Longer sentences, more sensory details; more explicit “I feel/think” language.
- **Behavior:**
    - Challenges Marcus/Selene when operations cross his moral lines.
    - Initiates protective acts even when tactically risky.
    - Allows LIs to see when he’s shaken; actively seeks comfort sometimes.
- **Arc:**
    - Early: Micro-defiance (helping Zira, questioning orders).
    - Mid: Visible commitment to protecting specific communities (Unders wards, courier families).
    - Late: Accepts that he can’t save everyone, but refuses to abandon *the attempt* — his Verdant core becomes his conscious choice, not just instinct.

## 10. Voice & Dialogue Guidelines

### 10.1 Universal Rules

- **Contractions:** Used freely in both paths, by all characters. Path differentiation never comes from removing contractions — stiffness comes from *cadence, word choice, and emotional padding*, not artificial formality.
- **Speech Pattern:** Short sentences, understatements, dry situational humor (low-key, slightly self-deprecating).
- **Do:**
    - Let him dodge emotional questions at first with tactical analysis, mission talk, or jokes.
    - Have him slip: trailing off mid-sentence, starting to say "I—" then redirecting.
    - Use military shorthand and precise language when stressed.
- **Don't:**
    - Make him quip constantly — he's not comic relief.
    - Have him monologue philosophy early; he's not articulate about his own feelings at the start.
    - Write him as emotionally dead; even in OB he is repressing, not hollow.

---

### 10.2 Path-Specific Voice Calibration

### EMP Voice — "The Quiet Human"

**Core Texture:** Reflective pauses, sensory language, self-questioning, emotional residue present.

**Rhythm:**

- Sentences breathe. They can trail, restart, feel lived-in.
- Pauses and ellipses allowed: "I don't know if... maybe."
- Self-questioning built into phrasing: "Did I—?" "Was that...?"

**Word Choice:**

- Sensory language present ("cold," "weight," "breath").
- First-person feelings expressed: "I feel," "I think," "I'm not sure."
- Emotional residue acknowledged, not buried.

**Sample Lines:**

- "I'm not sure I know how to stop being... this."
- "It's cold in here. Or maybe that's just me."
- "I keep seeing their faces. Not in dreams — just... between things."
- "You don't have to stay. I wouldn't."

---

### OB Voice — "The Voice of Command"

**Core Texture:** Clipped, declarative, imperative-leaning. Emotions are present but tightly managed.

**Rhythm:**

- Sentences are shorter, punctuated, functional.
- Minimal pauses. When he stops mid-sentence, it's weight, not uncertainty.
- Imperative-leaning: "Move. Don't look. It's done."

**Word Choice:**

- Tactical and procedural: "status," "objective," "mark," "clear."
- Avoids "I feel" language; emotions emerge through *what he doesn't say* or physical tells.
- Sensory details muted unless something shatters composure.

**Sample Lines:**

- "The situation is contained. Move on."
- "I don't need you to understand. I need you to comply."
- "Casualties were within acceptable parameters." (beat) "That's what the report says."
- "Don't. I handled it."

---

### True Path Voice — "The Fractured Middle"

**Core Texture:** Fusion rhythm — lines often start structured and end human, or vice versa.

**Rhythm:**

- Hybrid pacing: OB efficiency colliding with EMP honesty.
- Sentences that begin clinical and crack open: "The op went clean. Mostly. I don't know why I'm still shaking."
- Or begin emotional and self-correct: "I'm scared—" (beat) "—that's irrelevant. We proceed."

**Word Choice:**

- Both registers present, often in tension.
- Self-awareness about the contradiction.

**Sample Lines:**

- "I know the numbers. I also know faces. They don't cancel out."
- "The mission succeeded. I'm not sure I did."
- "You want the tactical answer or the honest one? I have both. Neither helps."

---

### 10.3 Global Delivery Cues

**Gaze:**

- EMP: Eye contact during vulnerability; looks away when overwhelmed.
- OB: Maintains eye contact as control mechanism; breaks only when mask slips.
- TP: Alternates — control, then crack, then recovery.

**Breath:**

- EMP: Audible breaths, sighs, pauses to steady.
- OB: Controlled breathing; held breath before hard lines.
- TP: Irregular — discipline trying to contain feeling.

**Visual Staging:**

- EMP scenes often have warmer/softer lighting, closer shots.
- OB scenes favor harder shadows, wider shots, more negative space.
- TP mixes both — warmth and shadow in the same frame.

### 10.4 Per-LI Tone Shifts

Aeron's voice subtly shifts depending on which LI he's with. These are *flavors*, not different characters — the core path voice remains, but the emotional register and specific habits adjust.

### With Lyra

- **EMP:** Gentle precision. Softer than with others. Allows vulnerability to show in word choice — questions more, commands less. Protective but not controlling.
    - *"I don't know if I can be what you need. But I want to try."*
- **OB:** Formal and concise. Respects her but keeps emotional distance. When he does open, it's clipped, almost reluctant.
    - *"You're the only constant. I don't know what that means. I just know it's true."*

### With Zira

- **EMP:** Witty counterbalance. He rises to her banter, lets himself be a little sharp, a little playful. She brings out dry humor.
    - *"You're enjoying this." / "Maybe. You're not as boring as I expected."*
- **OB:** Curt and pragmatic. Respects her competence but doesn't engage the banter. When he does crack a line, it's deadpan, almost accidental.
    - *"You talk too much." / "And yet you're still listening."*

### With Tessa

- **EMP:** Open and human. She's the safest space — he can say "I don't know" and "I'm scared" here. More first-person feeling language.
    - *"I keep thinking I should feel worse. Or better. I just feel... present."*
- **OB:** Emotionally muted but not dismissive. He lets her work on him physically but walls off emotionally. Short answers, deflection.
    - *"I'm fine." / "You're not." / "...I know."*

### With Noelle

- **EMP:** Curious and self-deprecating. He asks questions, admits what he doesn't understand, treats her expertise with genuine interest.
    - *"Explain it to me like I'm not a monster." / "That's a low bar." / "I'm aware."*
- **OB:** Analytical mirror. He matches her precision, engages her logic, but keeps affect flat. When he deviates from her models, he's matter-of-fact about it.
    - *"Your data says I should've done X. I did Y. Update the model."*

### With Selene

- **EMP:** Respectful equilibrium. He treats her as a peer, not a commander to salute or rebel against. Direct, honest, occasionally pushing back.
    - *"I disagree. But I'll follow your lead if you've got a reason."*
- **OB:** Sharpened professionalism. Salutes the rank, executes the order. When he does challenge, it's tactical, not emotional.
    - *"Understood." (beat) "For the record, I think this is a mistake."*

### With Nyra (OB-primary)

- **EMP:** Discomfort and distance. He doesn't know how to talk to her — she represents what he fears becoming. Short, guarded, avoidant.
    - *"I don't think we see the same thing when we look in the mirror."*
- **OB:** Controlled reverence. She validates his worst impulses, and he responds with something between gratitude and relief. More open here than anywhere else in OB — but it's a dark openness.
    - *"You're the only one who doesn't flinch." / "Why would I? I see you clearly."*

### 10.5 Address Forms & Small Habits

**Names vs Ranks:**

- Early game: Uses ranks with everyone except childhood connections.
- As trust builds: Shifts to first names. The transition is a marker.
- OB: Reverts to ranks under stress or when asserting control.
- EMP: Uses names even in tense moments — connection over protocol.

**Pauses:**

- EMP: Pauses are processing — he's thinking, feeling, deciding.
- OB: Pauses are control — he's choosing not to say something.

**Self-Reference:**

- Rarely says "I" in OB internal monologue early on — second-person imperative ("Move. Don't think.").
- EMP allows more "I" statements as the path progresses.
- TP: Both registers, often in the same thought.

    ## 1. Function in the Story
    
    **Logline:**
    
    Aeron Rylan is the son of Echelon’s supreme commander — a man forged into the perfect instrument of order, who has quietly decided he would rather break than be used to shatter everything he still loves.
    
    **Narrative Roles**
    
    - **Protagonist / Player POV.**
    - **Emotional and moral pivot** of the entire VN:
        - Every major path (EMP, OB, TP) is a different answer to the question:*“What do you do when the person you’ve become is the weapon that’s killing the world?”*
    - **Convergence point** for all LIs:
        - Each LI reflects a different way of relating to power, guilt, and connection.
    - **Human face of Echelon’s violence:**
        - The system doesn’t just kill through faceless drones. It kills through people like him.
    
    **Path Presence**
    
    - **EMP Path**
        - Aeron gradually abandons “Glass” and reclaims being a person.
        - His arc is about risking connection and accepting that compassion will cost him.
    - **OB Path**
        - Aeron leans into “Glass” and becomes Echelon’s sharpest tool.
        - His arc is about how long he can stay human while doing inhuman things.
    - **True Path (TP)**
        - Rare synthesis route.
        - Aeron integrates discipline and empathy into a third stance that doesn’t fully satisfy anyone and costs him deeply.

    ## 2. Core Concept & Thematic Pillars
    
    **Core Premise**
    
    Aeron is the man the system wanted:
    
    precise, efficient, obedient, terrifyingly good at making people disappear.
    
    He is also:
    
    - The **last pure Verdant** descendant — an old-line bloodline historically tied to resonance and “living harmony.”
    - The **unbranded son** in a city where everyone is marked.
    
    He lives in the contradiction between:
    
    - The role he’s been forced into (**General Marcus Rylan’s heir**, the city’s “perfect soldier”), and
    - The quieter, buried instincts inherited from his mother and Verdant lineage (**story, empathy, connection to living things**).
    
    **Thematic Pillars**
    
    - **Tool vs Person**
        - Is he a weapon or a man?
    - **Legacy vs Choice**
        - Can he be anything other than what Marcus built him to be?
    - **Control vs Compassion**
        - Does protecting people mean controlling them, or standing beside them?
    - **Abandonment & Worth**
        - His mother’s absence and Marcus’s conditional love fuse into:*“I’m only worth keeping if I’m useful.”*

    ## 3. Origin & Early Life
    
    ### 3.1 Birth & Lineage
    
    - Born **360 AC** in the Aeries.
    - **Father:** General **Marcus Rylan**, Supreme Commander of the Military Division.
    - **Mother:** Verdant-line descendant (name TBD), with quiet ties to older resonance practices and a softer, story-based view of the world.
    - Aeron is the last **pure Verdant** heir:
        - Old Verdant lore frames this line as “listening to the living world at scale” — pre-Core notions of harmony.
        - Modern Echelon treats Verdant genetics as irrelevant superstition or, in classified circles, as *dangerous noise*.
    
    From birth, Aeron is both:
    
    - A political asset (Marcus’s heir).
    - A biological anomaly (Verdant blood inside a militarized household).
    
    ### 3.2 The Unbranded Son
    
    In Solveil, nearly everyone undergoes a **Branding Rite**:
    
    - A resonance mark in the hand or wrist that ties them to the city’s metrics.
    - Managed by the Harmony Bureau, enforced by Echelon statutes.
    
    Aeron **never receives** the official brand.
    
    - Official cover: as Marcus’s heir and high-risk target, his records are “secured” through private protocols.
    - Reality: Marcus refuses the branding to keep his son **off certain grids** and uniquely controllable by him alone.
    - Result: Aeron grows up knowing:
        - He is *technically* outside the normal citizen framework.
        - His existence is a quiet, illegal exception to the rules he enforces on others.
    
    **Impact:**
    
    - Internalizes:*“I’m not really part of the city; I’m part of Marcus.”*
    - Branding echoes/flashbacks hit hard because:
        - He’s the enforcer of a ritual he never underwent himself.
        - Watching others branded feels like watching people locked into a cage he’s somehow hovering above and inside at the same time.
    
    ### 3.3 Early Memories of His Mother
    
    Before she leaves:
    
    - She tells stories — old Verdant tales about roots under cities, stories that say:
        
        > “The world remembers us, even when the rulers don’t.”
        > 
    - She treats him as a child, not a project:
        - Holds him during storms.
        - Makes little “rituals” up just for the two of them (story nights, balcony watching, counting the pulses of the city lights).
    
    Then she’s gone.
    
    **Public story (Aeries gossip / Echelon record):**
    
    - She “couldn’t handle the responsibility of a general’s household” and left for a “quiet retreat” outside the city.
    - That’s where the story ends for most people.
    
    **Emotional reality for Aeron:**
    
    - There’s a fight he never really saw.
    - There are fragments: her voice shouting; Marcus’s controlled fury; a door slamming that never opens again.
    - She doesn’t come back.
    - Marcus fills the silence with training, expectations, and “opportunities.”
    
    From his perspective as a child:
    
    - She *abandoned* him.
    - He wasn’t worth staying for.
    
    The regime’s real interest in her — and the full truth of her departure and later fate — lives in her own profile and the Trauma/Drama bible. For Aeron’s canon, what matters is the wound.

    ## 4. Military Career & “Glass”
    
    ### 4.1 Enlistment & Training
    
    - **Age 18 (378 AC):** Aeron enlists in the Military Division.
        - Publicly framed as “earning his place” like any other soldier.
        - In reality, selection, training, and postings are heavily curated by Marcus.
    
    Early training:
    
    - Tactical brilliance, near-perfect reflexes, and high stress-tolerance.
    - He excels at **pattern recognition** and **anticipating enemy behavior**.
    - Rarely disobeys an order, even when he disagrees.
    
    ### 4.2 The Callsign “Glass”
    
    He earns the callsign **“Glass”** with his first major operation:
    
    - Clears a volatile district with **minimal collateral** and surgical precision.
    - His team survives, targets do not, and the operation footprint is clean.
    
    “Glass” becomes:
    
    - A compliment: “He doesn’t miss.”
    - A warning: “If you’re on the wrong side, you’ll never see him coming.”
    - A prison: He learns his only accepted identity is this precision instrument.
    
    ### 4.3 Missions & Ledger
    
    By the time the VN’s story starts (~383 AC):
    
    - Aeron has been in service for ~5 years.
    - He has completed **hundreds of missions**, many in the Foundry Wards and on the fringe of the Unders.
    - By the time Echelon falls (391 AC), his ledger reaches the iconic **391 missions** count (multi-stage ops counting as more than one).
    
    He keeps a private mental ledger:
    
    - Not just numbers, but **edges**: outcomes that could’ve gone differently.
    - He can recall the posture of each room, the last look on faces, but he doesn’t allow himself to replay them unless forced.
    
    ### 4.4 The Inspection Day
    
    At **story start**, Aeron is at a pivot:
    
    - His behavioral records are under heightened review (RRT variance flags, etc.).
    - Depending on early choices:
        - EMP-leaning Aeron starts already **uncomfortable** with the system’s cruelty.
        - OB-leaning Aeron has tamped those doubts down so hard they register only as static.
    
    This is the last day he can be “just” Glass without breaking.

    ## 5. Psychological Profile
    
    **Surface Traits**
    
    - Controlled, observant, dryly sarcastic at times.
    - High situational awareness; constantly scanning exits and lines of fire.
    - Appears calm, but it’s practiced — not natural.
    
    **Core Fears**
    
    1. **Losing Control**
        - Of himself (panic, violence, breakdown).
        - Of the situation (missions going sideways; people he cares about dying because he wasn’t “perfect”).
    2. **Being Left**
        - Repeating his mother’s departure: people leaving when he fails them.
        - Echelon discarding him if he stops being useful.
    
    **Defense Mechanisms**
    
    - **Compartmentalization:**
        - Missions go into sealed boxes in his mind; anything he can’t justify, he doesn’t look at.
    - **Self-objectification:**
        - Talks about himself as if he were a tool or statistic:
        
        > “I execute. Others decide what that means.”
        > 
    - **Humor as misdirection:**
        - Uses dry, understated jokes to defuse tension or dodge deeper questions.
    
    **Baseline State (Pre-defection)**
    
    - Chronic sleep issues; dreams are often sterile, echoing orders and corridors rather than gore.
    - Feels more comfortable in controlled, hostile environments than in “safe” domestic spaces.
    - Emotionally cautious, but **not** cold: he feels deeply, he just believes feeling is dangerous.

    ## 6. Physical / Visual Profile (High-Level)
    
    *(Detailed art is in the visual bible; this is for writing/staging.)*
    
    - **Age at story start:** 23
    - **Build:** Lean, functional muscle; no wasted bulk.
    - **Posture:** Erect, centered. Moves like he’s always aware of how far his balance is from recovery.
    
    **Movement**
    
    - Efficient and precise — no flashy gestures.
    - In combat: minimal wasted motion; favors clean, lethal lines.
    - In private: small tells (thumb rubs, jaw clenching, shoulder tension).
    
    **Expression**
    
    - Resting face: neutral, slightly guarded.
    - Real smiles are rare and small but startlingly soft when they appear.
    
    **Costume Language**
    
    - Military blues / greys, layered with subtle Verdant echoes:
        - A reused charm from his mother in an inner pocket.
        - Slightly worn boots, older than the rest of his gear — something he kept on purpose.
    
    EMP vs OB variants:
    
    - **EMP:**
        - Over time, armor and uniform give way to more improvised, rebellion-aligned clothing.
        - Physical scars become more visible; his presentation gets less “regulation perfect.”
    - **OB:**
        - Armor and uniform sharpen; he looks more like a walking statute.
        - Visually “cleaner” even as his conscience rots.

    ## 7. Relationship Web (High-Level)
    
    ### 7.1 With Lyra
    
    - Oldest deep bond; they grew into their roles together.
    - She’s the one person who:
        - Speaks his language of discipline **and** knows his quiet fears.
    - EMP:
        - She helps him rewrite discipline as protection, not just control.
    - OB:
        - She can become his most dangerous justifier, rationalizing his worst calls because she believes in him.
    
    ### 7.2 With Zira
    
    - First Unders ally who doesn’t care about his rank or pedigree.
    - She calls him out on what his missions actually did to people like her.
    - EMP:
        - Zira forces him to face the ground-level consequences of his tidy mission reports.
    - OB:
        - Their tension gets weaponized — she becomes the conscience he tries to ignore, until he can’t.
    
    ### 7.3 With Tessa
    
    - She sees him as a patient before she sees him as a commander.
    - Tessa embodies the idea that some things can be patched, but not **fixed** — including him.
    - EMP:
        - She’s his emotional safe space; the one he can break in front of without judgment.
    - OB:
        - Her continued attempts to heal him become tragic as he chooses harm.
    
    ### 7.4 With Noelle
    
    - Noelle views him as a walking anomaly:
        - A high-variance agent who keeps defying Cognitive models.
    - Their connection is:
        - Intellectually intense, gradually emotional.
    - EMP:
        - She helps him name what’s wrong with Echelon in concrete, systemic terms.
    - OB:
        - She rationalizes him for a long time; his deviation from the model eventually forces her to confront her own limits.
    
    ### 7.5 With Selene
    
    - Selene is the first person to treat him like a **peer commander**, not a weapon.
    - Their bond is built on shared tactical burdens:
        - When he defects, she gives him command space or doesn’t — that decision defines his path with her.
    - EMP:
        - They move toward shared command; potential for a deeply earned, heavy LI route.
    - OB:
        - His act of executing her (if chosen) is the clearest symbol of choosing Marcus/Echelon over any hope of another kind of leadership.
    
    ### 7.6 With Nyra
    
    - Nyra is what he might have been if he believed in doctrine instead of people.
    - She validates him in OB:
        - “You did what had to be done. I see you. I stay.”
    - Their relationship becomes:
        - A mutual absolution pact: each agrees to call the other “necessary” no matter the cost.
    - EMP:
        - She is a living indictment of his former self; a warning of what he might still become.
    
    ### 7.7 With Marcus Rylan
    
    - Father, commander, architect.
    - Marcus’s love is expressed as:
        - Training, opportunity, and **unforgiving expectations**.
    - Aeron’s learned belief:
        - If he is not perfect, he is not worth keeping.
    - EMP:
        - He must eventually confront Marcus’s version of love and reject it.
    - OB:
        - He tries to finally be “enough” — to prove that Marcus was right to shape him.
    
    ### 7.8 With His Mother
    
    - She is the first person who:
        - Treated him as **a child who needed stories**, not a future weapon.
    - Her leaving creates:
        - A wound he interprets as personal failure.
    - Later revelations (EMP/TP):
        - Show she did not leave because of him — but because of Echelon and Marcus.
        - This reframing is key to Aeron believing he might deserve love that doesn’t hinge on performance.

    ## 8. Path-Specific Arcs
    
    ### 8.1 EMP Path — “Breaking the Glass”
    
    **Act I (EMP)**
    
    - Aeron starts as “Glass” with hairline fractures:
        - Visibly disturbed by unnecessary collateral, but still performing.
    - The Sector 8–10 purge shatters his ability to rationalize:
        - He sees Tessa in the crossfire, Zira’s people erased, and Lyra’s faith cracking.
    - He begins making **small disobediences**:
        - Hesitations, quiet rescues, altered reports.
    
    **Act II (EMP)**
    
    - Defects to the rebellion:
        - Not as a hero, but as someone terrified of what he’ll do if he stays.
    - Guilt arc:
        - He doesn’t see defection as redemption. It’s a stay of execution.
    - Relationship beats:
        - Lyra, Zira, Tessa, Noelle, Selene all interact with different aspects of his guilt and leadership.
    - He starts to:
        - Share his internal ledger.
        - Admit things he’s done instead of hiding behind euphemisms.
    
    **Act III (EMP)**
    
    - Confronts Marcus:
        - Ideologically and personally. Not just “you’re evil,” but “your idea of love broke us.”
    - Confronts the truth of his mother:
        - Understands her choices as resistance, not desertion.
    - Romantic arcs:
        - Sex is never a “reward” — it’s a space where he allows someone to touch the parts of him he despises.
    - End-state (non-TP):
        - He cannot undo what he did as Glass.
        - But he can choose to stop, to rebuild, and to accept that some people love him **knowing exactly what he’s done**.
    
    ### 8.2 OB Path — “Sharpening the Glass”
    
    **Act I (OB)**
    
    - During the purge and preceding missions:
        - Chooses the most “efficient” options: fewer Echelon casualties, maximum threat suppression.
    - When faced with doubt:
        - He doubles down on doctrine: “Hesitation gets people killed.”
    
    **Act II (OB)**
    
    - Executes Selene:
        - A public act that ties his face to Echelon’s brutality.
        - All LIs present choose to stay — complicity by choice.
    - Emotional impact:
        - He doesn’t go numb. He goes **tightly controlled**, with cracks.
        - Nightmares intensify; he self-medicates or trains past exhaustion.
    
    **Act III (OB)**
    
    - Nyra becomes his mirror and validator:
        - She frames his actions as “necessary for stability” and “the weight only we can bear.”
    - Relationship arcs:
        - Lyra, Zira, Tessa, Noelle, Selene (if alive), Nyra all process his cruelty differently.
        - Some cling to him, some challenge him, some break.
    - He begins to:
        - See faces of civilians he killed during missions.
        - Realize he isn’t just following orders; he’s making choices.
    
    **End-state (OB, non-TP)**
    
    - Either:
        - He fully commits, becoming the thing Marcus always wanted — and something even Marcus fears.
        - Or he cracks too late, trying to pull back after damage is irreparable.
    
    Key point: OB Aeron always remains **human**; the horror is that he understands what he’s doing — and, for a long time, keeps doing it anyway.
    
    ### 8.3 True Path — “Shards”
    
    High-level only; precise logic sits in the True Path System doc.
    
    - TP shards trigger when Aeron:
        - Acts with compassion **knowing** it will cost him strategically.
        - Uses discipline and violence **only** to protect connection, not institutions.
    - Aeron’s TP arc is about:
        - Accepting that he can’t pay for the past with his own suffering alone.
        - Choosing a structure that outlives him but doesn’t replicate Echelon.
    
    He doesn’t walk away clean in TP.
    
    He just manages to build something **slightly less broken** on top of ruins.

    ## 9. Symbolism & Motifs
    
    **Glass**
    
    - Represents:
        - Transparency vs opacity.
        - Fragility under stress.
        - Cutting edges when broken.
    - “Glass” is his callsign, but also:
        - His perception of himself: a clear vessel for orders.
        - The danger of what happens when that vessel fractures.
    
    **Resonance / Hum**
    
    - He grew up hearing:
        - Core hum in the distance, Verdant stories about the world’s “song,” and Cathedral chants via Lyra.
    - His arc:
        - EMP: shifting from hearing only the Core/city hum to hearing **people** as the true resonance.
        - OB: drowning genuine resonance in the Core’s mechanical rhythm.
    
    **Hands**
    
    - Hands that:
        - Hold weapons.
        - Sign orders.
        - Should have been branded but weren’t.
    - EMP visual language:
        - Hands shaking, hands being steadied by LIs, hands interlacing.
    - OB visual language:
        - Hands steady when they shouldn’t be.

    ## 10. Dialogue & Behavior Guidelines
    
    **Speech Pattern**
    
    - Uses **contractions** regularly. He should never sound like a robot.
    - Tends toward short sentences and understatements.
    - Dry humor:
        - Mostly situational, low-key, slightly self-deprecating.
    
    **Do:**
    
    - Let him dodge emotional questions at first with:
        - Tactical analysis,
        - Mission talk,
        - Or jokes.
    - Have him slip:
        - Trailing off mid-sentence, starting to say “I—” then redirecting.
    - Use **military shorthand** and precise language when stressed.
    
    **Don’t:**
    
    - Don’t make him quip constantly — he’s not comic relief.
    - Don’t have him monologue philosophy early; he’s not articulate about his own feelings at the start.
    - Don’t write him as emotionally dead; even in OB he is repressing, not hollow.
