# 29_Kess_Marrow_Character_Profile[CANON]

**DRAFT — pending author review. Authored by Claude during the Kael-reveal architecture session.**

---

**Approximate age:** 31 in 390 AC

**Origin:** Outlands orphan, collected by Liora Rylan's network at age 5

**Role:** Liora's apprentice and designated successor; story-keeper, ledger-bearer, caretaker of Soren Rylan

## 1. Before Liora

Kess does not remember her birth family. Her first name is the name Liora gave her. Her surname is the name she chose at sixteen, when Liora told her she could have one. She picked *Marrow* because the laundry collective that had fed her the winter she turned seven had an Old Tongue word for the fat inside a bone and the word meant *what you cannot see that keeps you warm when nothing else does.* She liked the word. She kept it.

## 2. Twenty-six years of apprenticeship

Liora took her in at age five and began teaching her the story-keeper's discipline as soon as she could hold a quill. The story-keeper's discipline is not a single skill. It is the habit of keeping names the world has tried to erase, the habit of letting people go quiet when going quiet is what keeps them alive, and the habit of waiting — sometimes for years — before you tell any one person any one thing. Kess has been learning this habit for twenty-six years.

She inherited Liora's ledger of the six thousand names over a long, slow handover that began when she was twenty and was complete by the time she was thirty. The ledger is currently in her possession. She keeps it in a cedar box with a single iron lock. The lock has one key. Kess wears it.

## 3. Soren

Kess was twenty-four when Liora brought Soren back from Sector 10. She was the one who held the four-year-old the first night in the Outlands household, because Liora was writing the extraction report and Kess had two free arms. Since that night, Kess has been Soren's de facto older sister — not a mother, because Liora was the grandmother, but the person Soren went to when Liora was out on the ledger rounds. Kess taught Soren to read. Kess was there when Soren asked the first questions about her father. Kess knows every piece of what Liora knew about the Rylan family.

## 4. After Liora's execution

Liora was captured in early 390 AC. Before she was taken, she gave Kess two things: the ledger and the standing instruction to tell Selene Ward about Soren's survival. Kess routed the message to Selene through the network. Kess has not spoken to Selene directly and does not plan to. She respects the old commander but the story-keeper's discipline is not to compound connections unnecessarily.

Kess has been Soren's only guardian for several months by the time Aeron arrives in Act 5. She has had to teach an eleven-year-old to continue grieving her grandmother while preparing to meet the only living blood relative she has. Kess has done this work without showing Soren how much of it is work.

## 5. At the Act 5 meeting

Kess is present. She does not speak unless Soren asks her to speak. She is watching Aeron. She has the authority, at any point, to end the meeting — and she will, if Soren signals the request. Soren, trained by Liora, signals with her hands folded in her lap in a specific way. Kess has been watching for the signal since the meeting began. Soren has not given it.

If Soren decides to tell Aeron the full truth, Kess provides one small confirmation at the end — she is the one who can say, aloud, that the officer of record for Operation 391 was Aeron Rylan, Captain, Echelon service. Kess is the one who holds the administrative piece. Soren holds the memory piece. Between the two of them, the story is complete.
