# Naming System Audit

A review of the *Ashes of Solveil* naming layer (characters, places, factions, operations, rituals, objects). Surfaces weak spots, ambiguities, and amplification opportunities. Does **not** revisit what's already working — see Section 5 for a brief note on the spine.

Scope: editorial / design layer only. No code or scene changes implied.

---

## 1. Worth Resolving

These are real ambiguities, not nits.

### 1.1 The Kael / Tessa Kael collision

- **Aeron's brother** is named Kael (first name).
- **A primary love interest** is named Tessa Kael (surname).
- In a system where naming collisions otherwise carry meaning, this reads as either a hidden Rylan-family connection waiting to pay off, or an accidental collision.
- Either commit to the connection (huge reveal layered on top of the Soren arc) or rename one. The brother's first name is more load-bearing — it's the absent voice that speaks through Selene; it sits at the heart of the Act 4 EMP thesis. **Recommendation:** protect "Kael" as the brother; rename Tessa.

### 1.2 Operation Stillwater (formerly Verdant) — orphaned rename

- Canon notes the rename but does not specify *who* renamed it or *why* they knew to.
- Two readings are both narratively rich, but they point in opposite directions:
  - **Marcus renamed it.** Then it's a third twice-told burial of the Verdant name (alongside Kael's "suicide" and Aeron's "fate"). The pattern hardens. Marcus has been suppressing the Verdant signifier for decades.
  - **Selene renamed it.** Then she knew about the bloodline lore much earlier than canon currently implies. This has structural consequences for what she has been protecting.
- Pick one. The detail is currently free-floating in a system where everything else is anchored.

### 1.3 Rhea Vestin — overstuffed or underused

- **Rhea** = Greek titaness, mother of the Olympians.
- **Vestin** = evokes Vestal, Roman flame-keeper.
- Neither resonance currently appears to be doing scene-level work in the canon.
- If she is not maternally coded narratively, "Rhea" reaches for a load it doesn't carry.
- If she is not Order Division (the division whose blade is **Veilbreaker**, which would rhyme with Vestal flame-keeping), "Vestin" is inert.
- **Resolution path:** Either lean into both readings (give her a "burning the children to save them" beat that activates *Rhea-as-mother* and *Vestin-as-flame-keeper* simultaneously) or simplify her name. The current state has her name reaching past her function.

## 2. Could Be Sharpened

These are not problems. They are unevennesses in a system that is otherwise tight.

### 2.1 Zira's name is the lightest in the cast

- Hebrew/Arabic/Swahili etymological options exist but none anchor strongly.
- The callsign **Ash-9** does real work (Foundry numerology, the burned thing). The personal name does not.
- In a cast where Aeron / Lyra / Liora / Selene / Tessa / Marcus all carry etymological weight, "Zira" stands out as unloaded.
- **Two amplification paths:**
  1. Lean on **Ash-9** harder so it becomes her real name in the rebellion's mouth — itself thematic (the Unders renaming themselves).
  2. Give "Zira" an in-world etymology. Her motif is "from before"; her name could be from before too — an Old Tongue word, or a survival from a pre-Echelon language the Unders kept.

### 2.2 "Mark of Obedience" is the thuddy name in a layered system

- Compare to its peers:
  - **Veilbreaker** — named to break Solveil; performs double duty.
  - **The Lieutenant's Oath** — legal/sacred double meaning.
  - **The Branding** — cattle metaphor as ritual.
- "Mark of Obedience" only does one thing. It tells you what it is instead of meaning it.
- **Possible reframings:** *the Seal*, *the Stillpoint* (Cathedral resonance), *the Cut*. Anything with a second meaning the regime thinks it has chosen for one reason and the player can read for another.

### 2.3 The Six are 2/6 named

- **Anayet** (witness-of-hurt) and **Seren** (hold what will break you) are present in canon.
- The other four mercies are referenced but unnamed.
- In a system where Old Tongue carries thematic weight, leaving 4/6 of the central religious paradigm unnamed leaves real estate fallow.
- **Amplification:** Name all six. The math already works for mapping them to the six love interests (Lyra / Tessa / Zira / Noelle / Selene / Nyra), which would make the Cathedral's older mercy paradigm structurally identical to the romance ensemble. Whether that mapping is desirable is a separate decision; the naming is overdue regardless.

### 2.4 "Verdant" and "Ethereal" are modern English in a classical-rooted cast

- The cast roots are Greek / Latin / Hebrew / Welsh: Aeron, Lyra, Liora, Marcus, Selene, Tessa, Soren, Rhea.
- The two bloodline names that carry the *mythological* weight are derived from late Latin via Old French — abstractions where everything else is a name.
- This may be intentional (the bloodlines are universal forces; named for qualities, not references). But it is the one place the linguistic register breaks.
- **Amplification:** Give each bloodline an in-world etymology. A phrase from a lost text. A corrupted older name. What did the Cathedral *used* to call Ethereals before the Bureau standardized it? Does Liora know? Does Lyra? This is free thematic gold.

## 3. Small Things

Low priority. Worth noting only because the system is otherwise so tight.

- **"Mind & Hands"** for TR&D is the only doubled body-part division name. The other four are singular (Sword, Hand, Skeleton, Voice). Either lean into it (TR&D *is* internally fractured into 5 sub-directorates — that's the doubling earning itself) or simplify to "the Hands" or "the Mind."

- **The Council of Archons is eleven.** Currently coincidental. Could be made explicit: *whose seat is empty?* (Liora's, in some symbolic sense?) The "apostles minus one, the seat that's never filled" reading is free if you want it.

- **Aris** carries less weight than her narrative function deserves. Selene's mother — the martyr whose burned gloves become Selene's command-self — is load-bearing enough to deserve a name as anchored as Liora's. "Aris" (best / Ares-shortened) is a quick gloss for a structurally important character.

- **Kess Marrow** — *Marrow* is doing all the work. *Kess* is fine, just lighter than its surname. Asymmetric, not broken.

## 4. Open Questions for the Author

Issues where the answer determines the recommendation:

1. Is Tessa Kael related to Aeron's brother, or is the surname a collision?
2. Who renamed Operation Verdant to Stillwater, and when?
3. Is Rhea Vestin Order Division or Military? Does her arrival activate maternal symbolism in scenes?
4. Should Ash-9 become Zira's primary name in the rebellion's mouth?
5. Are the four unnamed mercies in the Six waiting for names, or are Anayet and Seren the only two that ever existed?
6. Do "Verdant" and "Ethereal" have older in-world names that the Cathedral or Liora's archive preserves?
7. Council of Archons = 11 — is the missing twelfth meaningful?

## 5. What Is Not At Issue

Brief note for context. The following are working and should not be touched:

- The **sol** spine (Solveil / Solace / Liora / Saint Solenne).
- **Marcus = Mars / Aeron = slaughter / Liora = my light** as the family's central conflict encoded in three names.
- **Lyra → Nyra** as music-with-and-without-L.
- **Veilbreaker** named to destroy the city it serves.
- **Echelon** (ladder) as the regime's architecture in its own name.
- **Aeries** (eagles' nests) as predator-housing.
- **Tessa = tessera** (four facets — Compassion / Resilience / Truth / Purpose) as the Harmonize ending's geometry.
- **Soren** (from *Severus*, "the one who tests") as the niece who runs the survival-test.
- **Selene / Endymion** as Selene / Kael.
- **Cell-Theta** (Greek θ for *thanatos*) as a death-coded birthplace.
- **Operation 391** numerology (mission count / atrocity / fall-of-Echelon year share a number; 3+9+1=13).

These are anchored and earning. The audit above is calibrated against this baseline.
